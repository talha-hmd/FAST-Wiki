Finances

FAST Scholarships — Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA & Ihsan Trust

FAST students can explore multiple financial support channels, including government scholarships, university-linked aid, external trusts, and merit-based tuition relief. The important thing is not to depend on only one option. Apply broadly, verify every deadline, and confirm whether different scholarships can be combined.

Scholarships vs Financial Assistance

Before comparing options, understand the difference between scholarships and Financial Assistance. FAST Financial Assistance is usually need-based and commonly repayable as Qarz-e-Hasana. Scholarships may work differently depending on the scheme.

Some scholarships are need-based, some are merit-based, and some are government-funded. Their eligibility rules, deadlines, documents, and continuation requirements can be completely different.

Financial Assistance

Usually need-based and often repayable as Qarz-e-Hasana.

Scholarship

May be need-based, merit-based, government-funded, or external.

Merit Relief

Usually awarded to top performers or top entry-test scorers.

Important: Do not assume every scholarship is free, automatic, or combinable. Confirm the latest rules from FAST accounts, the scholarship office, or the official scheme portal.

Punjab Honhaar Scholarship

The Punjab Honhaar Scholarship is a government scholarship channel intended to support eligible students in higher education. For students at private universities, the merit bar can be very high, so eligibility does not always mean selection.

Student reports suggest that implementation at FAST has sometimes been confusing, with delays, unclear coverage, or differences between what students expected and what was actually processed. Because of that, students should track both the official government portal and FAST's own communication.

Government Scheme

Run through government scholarship policy, not only through FAST.

High Merit Bar

Private-university merit requirements can be extremely competitive.

Verify Coverage

Confirm whether it covers full tuition, partial tuition, or a delayed amount.

Publishing note: Honhaar rules and implementation can change. Before admission season, verify current eligibility, coverage, and FAST-specific processing from official sources.

PEEF Scholarship

PEEF, the Punjab Educational Endowment Fund, is another Punjab-government scholarship channel. It is usually relevant for Punjab-domiciled students who meet income and academic conditions.

Students should ask FAST's HR, accounts, or scholarship-related office about the application window at the start of the academic year. Missing the deadline can mean waiting for the next cycle.

  • Usually relevant for Punjab-domiciled students.
  • Family income limit may apply.
  • Academic record may be considered.
  • Application is usually handled around the start of the academic year.
  • Confirm process through FAST's relevant campus office.
Tip: Keep domicile, income proof, academic certificates, CNIC/B-Form, and fee documents ready before scholarship season starts.

OSAF

OSAF is listed in FAST's aid ecosystem as another possible support channel. Students may receive an email or notice after the semester starts inviting applications.

The key issue with OSAF is eligibility and overlap. If you are already receiving another scholarship or financial support, you may or may not be eligible. Always confirm this directly from the accounts office.

University-Linked Aid

Usually communicated through FAST channels.

Email Notice

Students may be invited to apply after the semester begins.

Overlap Check

Confirm whether it can be combined with another scholarship.

FANA & Ihsan Trust

FANA and Ihsan Trust are additional need-based support channels that may help students manage FAST's fee burden. Ihsan Trust is an external charitable trust, so its process may differ from FAST's internal FA system.

These options should not be ignored, especially by students whose family cannot manage the full fee. But coverage, eligibility, deadlines, and repayment conditions should be confirmed directly.

Channel Type What to Confirm
FANA Need-based support channel Eligibility, coverage amount, deadline, and continuation rules.
Ihsan Trust External charitable trust Application process, repayment terms if any, and required documents.
FAST FA Need-based university assistance Whether it can be combined with FANA, Ihsan Trust, or other scholarships.
Important: External aid can have its own rules. Do not assume it follows the same process as FAST Financial Assistance.

Merit-Based Tuition Relief

FAST may offer merit-based tuition relief to top entrants, especially students who perform exceptionally well in the admission process. This is different from need-based FA because it is based on performance rather than family income.

For top entry-test scorers, tuition relief can be extremely valuable because it usually does not create the same repayment burden as Qarz-e-Hasana.

Performance-Based

Awarded to top scorers or top-ranked entrants.

No Repayment

Usually better than repayable assistance if you qualify.

Highly Competitive

Requires exceptional admission performance.

Best case: Merit-based relief is one of the cleanest forms of support because it reduces cost without creating post-graduation debt.

Which Options Should You Apply For?

The safest strategy is to apply to every channel you may qualify for. Do not wait for one scholarship result before applying to another. Scholarship decisions can be delayed, partial, or uncertain.

Your goal is to create multiple chances of support. Even if one option is rejected or delayed, another may still help.

1

Apply for FAST Financial Assistance during admission if you need support.

2

Check Honhaar eligibility through official government and FAST channels.

3

Ask about PEEF at the start of the academic year if you are eligible.

4

Watch for OSAF notices or emails after the semester starts.

5

Ask accounts or FA office about FANA and Ihsan Trust options.

Rule: Apply broadly, but confirm whether different scholarships can be combined before depending on multiple awards.

Documents to Keep Ready

Scholarship documents vary by scheme, but many applications ask for similar proof. Preparing these early can save time when deadlines are short.

  • CNIC or B-Form
  • Father's or guardian's CNIC
  • Academic result cards
  • FAST admission or enrollment proof
  • Fee challan or fee structure proof
  • Domicile, if required
  • Income certificate or salary slip
  • Bank statement
  • Electricity and gas bills
  • Rent agreement or house ownership details, if needed
  • Scholarship application form
  • Passport-size photographs
Tip: Make a scanned folder of all important documents. Keep both PDF and image versions ready.

Questions to Ask the Accounts Office

Before relying on any scholarship, ask the university office clear questions. Verbal assumptions can create problems later, especially if scholarship funds are delayed or partially approved.

  • Is this scholarship currently available at FAST?
  • What is the exact deadline?
  • Is it full tuition, partial tuition, or fixed amount?
  • Will the amount be adjusted before or after fee payment?
  • Can it be combined with FAST FA?
  • Can it be combined with Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA, or Ihsan Trust?
  • What CGPA or academic standing is required to continue it?
  • What happens if funds are delayed?
  • Will I need to pay first and get reimbursed later?

Common Mistakes Students Make

Scholarship mistakes usually happen because students wait too long, trust informal posts, or assume one scholarship will solve everything.

  • Applying only to one scholarship channel.
  • Missing deadlines because documents were not ready.
  • Assuming government scholarship funds will arrive immediately.
  • Not checking whether two scholarships can be combined.
  • Confusing FAST FA with a free scholarship.
  • Ignoring merit-based relief opportunities.
  • Depending on old screenshots or senior stories without official confirmation.
  • Not asking what happens if the scholarship is delayed.

The Best Strategy

The best strategy is to treat funding like a portfolio. Do not depend on one source unless it is officially confirmed, documented, and reflected in your fee situation.

Apply for FAST FA if you need it. Apply for government scholarships if eligible. Ask about OSAF, FANA, and Ihsan Trust. If you are a top scorer, check merit-based relief. Keep a backup university plan ready until your financial position is actually confirmed.

Apply Early

Scholarship windows can be short, so documents should be ready before announcements.

Apply Broadly

One delayed scholarship should not destroy your entire fee plan.

Verify Officially

Always confirm eligibility, coverage, and combination rules from official sources.

Quick Summary

FAST students can explore multiple scholarship and aid channels: Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA, Ihsan Trust, FAST FA, and merit-based tuition relief. Each has different rules, deadlines, coverage, and continuation requirements.

The safest approach is to apply to every possible channel, keep documents ready, verify combination rules, and never depend on unofficial claims without checking with the relevant office.
