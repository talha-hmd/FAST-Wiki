How to Apply to FAST NUCES — Application Walkthrough

Applying to FAST is not difficult, but small mistakes in preferences, documents, or test-date selection can create problems later. This page walks you through the application process step by step so you know what to do before submitting your form.

Step 1 — Create Your Application Account

Register on the central admissions portal at nu.edu.pk/Admissions/HowToApply. Make sure to use a stable email address and phone number that you actively check—all official correspondence from the university lands here.

Tip: Keep your login details saved somewhere safe. You may need the same account again for test slips, result tracking, fee challans, or future updates.

Step 2 — Choose Programs and Campuses (Preference Ordering)

You list your desired programs and campuses in order of preference. This ordering structure is critical because FAST checks your aggregate score against your choice hierarchy:

First Preference

FAST grants your first preference automatically if your aggregate score clears its cutoff boundary condition.

Lower Preference

If your score clears a lower preference but not your first choice, you are accepted into that alternative choice with the status marked as "Eligible."

Denied Status

If your total calculation fails to clear the closing numbers for any of your specified choices, your application status is marked as "Denied."

Order matters: place your most-preferred program first, but limit it strictly to campuses you would genuinely attend. Being marked "Eligible" into a lower preference choice still binds you to that selection track.

Step 3 — Upload Documents

After selecting your preferences, upload your application documents carefully. These files include your Intermediate or Matric result cards (or your O/A-Level records paired with your official IBCC equivalence certificate), your CNIC or B-Form, standard passport photographs, and your domicile. If you are seeking aid in-flow during registration, submit your supporting financial assistance or scholarship documents here.

FAST admits students conditionally pending final academic results, which is standard practice across institutions. Your seat is confirmed once your final result card is issued, re-uploaded to the system, and satisfies the program's eligibility criteria.

Important: Upload clear images or PDFs. Blurry, cropped, or incorrect documents can delay your application or create verification issues.

Step 4 — Pay the Application / Processing Fee

Once your application details are complete, pay the processing fee through the designated methods provided inside the portal. Please note that the admission processing fee is completely non-refundable at FAST and every peer institution.

Keep your payment receipt safe—you will need to input or refer to the transaction reference number later during the cycle.

Step 5 — Select Your NU Test Sitting

FAST allows you to self-select your test date within the registration system. Later test dates offer more preparation time, meaning they are highly preferred and fill up first.

Step 6 — Track Results and the Merit Chart

FAST does not publish a standard ranked merit list containing student serial numbers. Instead, it publishes a Selected / Eligible / Denied status chart matching every campus-and-program intersection.

Any specific "closing merit" numbers floating around online on social media or forums are unofficial reconstructions built by students from self-reported data. Treat them purely as estimates, not as hard facts.
