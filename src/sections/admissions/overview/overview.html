FAST Admissions: Eligibility, Streams & How to Apply

This page gives you a clear starting point for FAST-NUCES admissions. It explains who can apply, which admission streams exist, where the official information lives, and how the admission calendar usually works.

Who Can Apply — Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility is defined on the central page nu.edu.pk/Admissions/EligibilityCriteria. A minimum intermediate / FSc / ICS percentage (or O/A-Level equivalence via IBCC) is required. The exact minimum percentage is set per program and per cycle — look up your target program on the EligibilityCriteria page above to get the current figure.

Subject combinations differ per program. Computing programs such as CS and AI require the maths-heavy combination (Maths + the relevant science electives); other programs have their own specific combinations listed clearly on that same page.

O/A-Level candidates must obtain IBCC equivalence before their academic record can be scored and parsed by the university processing system.

Important: Treat Reddit, WhatsApp, coaching academies, and even this site as commentary that must be checked against the central portal. Eligibility rules can change, so the official admissions portal should be your final source.

The Three Admission Streams (at a glance)

FAST admissions happen through three main streams, but they are not equal because each one competes in a completely separate merit pool.

NU Test

FAST's own entrance exam. Largest seat pool, lowest closing merit. Recommended default option.

SAT (SAT-I)

Separate merit list, smaller self-selecting pool, higher closing percentage requirement.

NTS NAT

Smallest reserved seat slice; historically requires a near-perfect score. Avoid unless your NAT score is near-perfect.

For a deeper breakdown of how these rules work, a full structural comparison lives on the Stream Comparison page.

The Admissions Calendar (shape, not dates)

FAST's exact dates shift year to year, but the general calendar sequence follows a predictable lifecycle structure:

1

Applications open in late spring on the central admissions portal.

2

NU Test sittings take place across the announced range during late June–July.

3

Results and initial merit charts are announced in approximately 2–3 weeks.

4

Selected students submit their fees before the deadline to lock down their admission status.

5

University academic sessions and formal classes begin.

Reminder: Exact dates are explicitly updated and published on the Admission Schedule page at nu.edu.pk/Admissions/Schedule. Do not assume last year's calendar timeline will repeat exactly.
