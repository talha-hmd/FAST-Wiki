The FAST NU Test — Sections, Weighting & How to Prep

The NU Test is FAST's own entrance test and, for most applicants, the safest admission route. This page explains the test sections, which parts matter most for computing applicants, how test-date selection works, and how to prepare without wasting time.

Test Composition

The FAST NU Test contains four major sections: Advanced Math, Basic Math, Analytical / IQ Reasoning, and English. These sections evaluate both your core academic preparation and your ability to process problems under strict time pressure.

Advanced Math

Covers higher-level mathematical concepts from your intermediate syllabi. For computing applicants, this forms a massive pillar of your testing profile.

Basic Math

Includes simpler arithmetic operations and structural word problems. The content structure requires extreme speed and rapid comprehension.

Analytical Reasoning

Tests pattern identification, logical puzzles, and spatial IQ. This section is highly responsive to consistent tactical preparation.

Important: FAST frequently revises its testing structures between cycles. The authoritative, official current split must always be verified on the active portal page at nu.edu.pk/Admissions/TestPattern.

Indicative Section Weighting

While FAST alters these parameters depending on the cycle, the commonly reported indicative historical weights across the section maps provide an ideal blueprint for planning your study allocation:

  • Advanced Math — Approximately 30%
  • Basic Math — Approximately 20%
  • Analytical Reasoning — Approximately 30%
  • English — Approximately 20%

Do not memorize these percentages blindly as fixed rules. Use them as an approximate planning baseline, but rely on the official portal link above for the active cycle's distribution.

Which Sections Decide Your Aggregate?

For most computing applicants, Advanced Math and Analytical Reasoning are the definitive swing sections. These are the areas where focused, highly targeted preparation will cause a disproportionate jump in your total merit computation.

Engineering-background students repeatedly report Basic Math word problems as their critical blind spot. The core weakness here is rarely raw calculation capacity, but rather sentence translation. The solution requires explicit practice mapping an English paragraph framework directly into a mathematical equation.

Advanced Math

High-impact swing section. Mastery over core calculus and intermediate principles forms the foundation of a safe aggregate.

Analytical/IQ

The most efficiently improvable block. It features finite, pattern-recognizable question models where a few weeks of consistent puzzle practice yields major score increases.

English

Generally acts as a steady baseline. While essential, it rarely functions as the primary score-changing element separating the applicant pools.

Sittings & The Self-Selecting Date Mechanic

FAST runs multiple test sittings spanning a set date range. Because later slots offer maximum preparation time, they are universally preferred by applicants and fill up almost instantly.

Complete your registration profile and pay the entry processing fee.

2

Access the date-selection window inside the central registration ecosystem.

3

Monitor the system across several days as new testing dates populate the drop-down selectors.

4

Avoid panic-locking an early slot in one shot; instead, check the portal iteratively to secure an optimal date.

How to Prepare (The Pre-Test Playbook)

The NU Test is competitive because of aggregate scaling pressure, not because the individual questions are impossibly complex. Your preparation roadmap should focus explicitly on high-yield, repeatable strategies:

  • Start your Advanced Math review directly from your standard intermediate and Calculus textbook banks.
  • Isolate Basic Math word problems and drill translation mechanics—transforming text blocks into clean formulas.
  • Dedicate a consistent study window to solve Analytical Reasoning puzzles daily from a reliable question bank for 4 to 6 weeks.
  • Sit for at least 2 to 3 full-length diagnostic mock exams under strict, authentic timed conditions.
Best use of time: Do not ignore the power of the Analytical section. Because it relies on consistent pattern logic, a systematic multi-week routine will turn this section into a guaranteed aggregate driver.
