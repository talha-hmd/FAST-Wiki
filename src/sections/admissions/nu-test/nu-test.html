FAST NU Entry Test Pattern 2026 — Sections & Strategy

The NU Test is FAST's own computer-based entrance exam and, for most applicants, the safest admission route. This page breaks down the exact test layout, section weights, strict testing center protocol rules, and field-tested preparation resources.

FAST NU Entry Test Pattern 2026

The computer-based evaluation structure contains four major sections: Advanced Math, Basic Math, Analytical Skills & IQ, and English. For computing program applicants (competing for seats costing Rs. 12,000 per credit hour), your final score is built on a highly specific distribution pattern:

Test Section Official Weightage Primary Core Focus Area
Advanced Mathematics 50% of Exam Intermediate HSSC core calculus, functions, trigonometry, and algebraic matrices.
Basic Mathematics 20% of Exam Arithmetic operations, ratios, percentages, and dense narrative word problems.
Analytical Skills & IQ 20% of Exam Logical puzzle deduction, sequential pattern identification, and spatial tracking.
English 10% of Exam Sentence completions, vocabulary relationships, error detections, and grammar syntax.
Official Pattern Sync: FAST occasionally adjusts testing layouts between admission cycles. The current 50/20/20/10 structure is official for the 2026 computing pool. Always check real-time system alerts at nu.edu.pk/Admissions/TestPattern.

Which Sections Decide Your Aggregate?

With Advanced Math making up half of the entire test, this single section acts as the primary gatekeeper for your aggregate score. It is a rapid speed game—you are evaluated on your ability to work through conceptual structures quickly without hitting performance bottlenecks.

Furthermore, intermediate students repeatedly report Basic Math word problems as an unexpected blind spot. The issue is rarely core calculation capacity, but sentence translation mechanics. Success requires deliberate practice breaking down an English paragraph frame directly into a clean mathematical equation.

The Absolute Calculator Ban: Scientific or standard calculators are strictly prohibited inside the university computer labs. Every equation, fraction conversion, or trigonometry derivation must be evaluated by hand using scratch paper provided at the center. Practice manual calculations throughout your preparation.
The Negative Marking Survival Rule: The test architecture implements a fractional penalty system that deducts -0.25 marks for every incorrect response. Seniors emphasize a strict rule of thumb: only choose an answer if you are at least 80% confident. Leaving a highly uncertain question blank yields 0 damage, whereas blind guessing is the fastest way to tank your aggregate.

Sittings & The Self-Selecting Date Mechanic

The university runs multiple test sessions spanning a multi-day testing block. Because later dates offer maximum study time, these blocks are highly preferred and fill up almost instantly once fees are processed.

How to Prepare (The Pre-Test Playbook)

The NU Test is challenging due to strict time limits, not because the questions are inherently impossible. Your preparation roadmap should focus on highly repeatable, efficient routines:

  • Review Advanced Math concepts directly from intermediate board textbooks, prioritizing calculus and functions.
  • Utilize Senior Resources: Seniors strongly recommend utilizing the online Recluze (Dr. Nauman) and Mathsflix (Hashim Zia) YouTube playlists. These lectures offer exceptional conceptual clarity for advanced math and logic frameworks.
  • Drill word problems to master translation mechanics, practicing converting paragraphs into algebraic formulas.
  • Solve daily IQ puzzles consistently for 4 to 6 weeks from a reliable bank to turn the Analytical section into a guaranteed aggregate booster.
  • Complete timed mock tests under authentic exam conditions to adapt your pacing before entering the testing center.
