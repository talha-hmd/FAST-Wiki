FAST vs NUST vs GIKI vs LUMS — Fees, Merit & Outcomes

Choosing a university is not only about which one sounds more prestigious. The better question is: which university fits your budget, career goal, GPA needs, and family situation? This page compares FAST, NUST, GIKI, and LUMS from a practical applicant point of view.

Where FAST Sits on Price

FAST is a private university, so it is usually more expensive than public-sector options like UET, PUCIT, or NED. However, it is generally below the elite-private ceiling of universities like LUMS and GIKI.

Compared with NUST, FAST can be broadly similar in computing tuition depending on program, campus, and current fee cycle. But the real cost is not just tuition. You also need to consider admission fees, security deposit, hostel, transport, repeat-course fees, late-payment fines, and other small charges.

Important: Fee figures change every year. Before publishing this page publicly, re-check every number from each university's official fee page.

The Charges That Bite Mid-Semester

Many students only compare tuition fee and ignore the small charges that appear later. These include late-payment fines, registration suspension rules, transcript charges, migration fees, hostel charges, summer course fee, and repeat-course billing.

This matters because a university that looks affordable at first can become stressful if your family is already close to the budget limit. For FAST specifically, repeats and summer courses can become important because of the stricter academic pressure.

  • Admission or processing fee
  • Security deposit
  • Semester tuition
  • Hostel and transport
  • Late-payment fines
  • Repeat-course fee
  • Summer course fee
  • Transcript, migration, and administrative charges

The Refund Window

Refund policy matters when you are holding one university offer while waiting for another. For example, a student may deposit FAST's fee to secure a seat while still waiting for NUST or LUMS.

Under the commonly referenced refund framework, the earlier you withdraw, the more fee you can recover. This makes timing very important.

1

Up to day 10: Usually the highest refund window.

2

Up to day 15: Refund amount starts reducing.

3

Up to day 20: Further reduction in refundable amount.

4

Up to day 30: Last meaningful refund window.

5

After day 30: Usually no refund.

Practical use: If you pay to secure a seat, track the refund dates carefully. Missing the refund window can cost a lot.

FAST's Main Advantage

FAST's biggest strength is its local computing reputation. In Pakistan's software market, the FAST tag can help clear CV filters and signal that the student has survived a tough academic environment.

For students who want a local software career after graduation, FAST can be a very strong choice if the fee is manageable. The workload is tough, but the brand has real value in local hiring.

Strong Local Tag

FAST is highly respected in Pakistan's computing industry.

Employer Signal

The academic grind creates a reputation for persistence and technical discipline.

Best Fit

Strong choice if your goal is a local tech career and your family can manage the cost.

When NUST, LUMS, GIKI, or Public Universities May Be Better

FAST is not automatically the best choice for every student. If your main goal is a funded master's abroad, then GPA, international recognition, research opportunities, and grading environment matter a lot.

In that case, universities with gentler grading or stronger international pipelines may be better for your long-term plan. Public universities can also be a smart choice if they protect your budget and allow you to build skills independently.

  • Choose FAST if your goal is a strong local computing career and the fee is manageable.
  • Consider NUST or LUMS if your main goal is international recognition or a funded master's abroad.
  • Consider GIKI if you can afford the residential cost and want that environment.
  • Consider public universities if affordability and GPA protection are major priorities.

The Strategic Upshot

The decision is not “which university is best?” The real decision is “which university is best for my specific goal and my specific budget?”

FAST is middle-tier on price compared to elite private universities, but top-tier in local employer preference for computing. NUST and LUMS may be better for students focused on international pathways. Public universities may be better for students who need affordability and GPA protection.

Final rule: Do not choose only by name. Choose by budget, career goal, GPA needs, and the kind of pressure you can realistically handle.

Quick Summary

FAST is a strong choice for local computing careers, but it is not cheap and the academic pressure is real. NUST and LUMS can be stronger for abroad-focused students. GIKI is respected but expensive. Public universities can be underrated if they help you save money and build skills without debt pressure.

The best university is not the one with the loudest reputation. It is the one that fits your end goal and your financial reality.
