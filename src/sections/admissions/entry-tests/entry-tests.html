FAST NU Entry Test Pattern 2026 — Stream Selection Strategy

One of the biggest FAST admission decisions is not just which campus or program you choose. It is also which admission stream you apply through. The NU Test, SAT, and NTS NAT do not compete in the exact same pool, so choosing the wrong stream can make admission much harder than it needs to be.

FAST Internal Test vs NAT Weightage (The Stream Decision)

Your single most consequential admissions choice is the stream selection, because each path competes for an entirely isolated seat pool with its own closing merit boundaries. Your scoring matrix is never judged globally, but strictly against alternative applicants applying through that identical data pipeline:

Admission Stream Seat Pool Allotment Testing Difficulty Metrics Aggregate Baseline Reality
NU Internal Test Bulk Seating Core (85%+) Advanced conceptual math and time-sensitive analytical reasoning modules. Bounded by strict testing protocol rules. The most forgiving closing criteria layout due to sheer depth of available campus slots. Safest route.
SAT Track Small Separate Slice Standard international layout. Scores must be dispatched to the university officially using Institution Code 4575. Extremely competitive separate merit list processing. Typically requires a strong profile (~1250+ to 1300+).
NTS NAT (National Aptitude Test) Paper-Thin Slice General standardized academic testing framework managed via the National Testing Service. The "NAT Trap"—Because the seat allocation block is so restricted, it routinely demands a near-perfect score to secure an offer.
Simple Rule of Thumb: Default to the NU Test stream. Use the SAT track only if you already hold a stellar score and accept the competitive nature of separate pool tracking. Avoid the NTS NAT pipeline entirely as a core admission pathway unless your starting score is near-flawless.

Closing Merit History: Unofficial Reconstructions

Because FAST does not publish a standard, public ranked leaderboard containing individual applicant indexes, closing-merit figures floating around social spaces are unofficial community tracking records. They are collected manually from student self-reported profile updates:

1

NU Test Track (Lahore Campus CS): Unofficial logs show closing aggregate numbers landing in the low-to-mid 70s (~73% base), illustrating why this remains your most accessible target option.

2

SAT Merit Track: Separate evaluation lists have closed at approximately ~85% for Computer Science structures and ~86% for specialized Artificial Intelligence tracks.

3

NAT Merit Track: Swings wildly due to the minimal seating block allocation. History highlights sudden +10% standard jumps and intense volatility depending entirely on small candidate spikes.

This is why senior students remind applicants never to choose an admission stream purely because the exam paper seems "easy." A moderately challenging test paired with thousands of open slots is structurally safer than an easier test layout fighting over a paper-thin seating quota.

The Testing Protocol: Hard Center Rules (Senior Advice)

Surviving the actual examination room requires complete alignment with FAST's strict test-center monitoring procedures. Breaking these boundaries can result in immediate candidate disqualification.

The Calculator Ban: Mathematical and scientific calculators are strictly prohibited inside the computer testing labs. Every single quantitative math, advanced calculus, or logic section must be computed manually using scratch paper provided by the invigilators. Train yourself to complete rapid fraction divisions and algebraic tracking by hand during your test prep.
The Negative Marking Risk: The computer test deducts -0.25 marks for every incorrect answer, so blind guessing can snowball into a serious aggregate drag. See The NU Test for the full negative-marking survival strategy (the 80%-confidence rule, leaving blanks, and more).
The Later Date Booking Warning: The registration engine allows you to pick your own exam slot. While choosing a late testing date (July 8–10) maximizes your preparation timeframe, seniors note that these specific blocks run out within hours of payment verifications updating. Lock your slot immediately once authorized; if you delay, you will be squeezed into crowded, early-stage testing blocks.
