FAST Glossary — Ragra, Sessionals, Absolutes, FA & More

FAST students use many terms that can confuse applicants and first-semester students. This glossary explains the most common admissions, academic, financial, campus-life, and career terms in simple language.

Admissions Terms

These are the terms you will hear during the admission process, merit discussion, and application stage.

Term Meaning
NU Test FAST's own entrance test. For most applicants, this is the recommended default admission stream.
NAT National Aptitude Test by NTS. For FAST, it usually has a tiny seat pool, so it can require a very high score.
SAT-I The standard SAT route used on a separate FAST merit list. It is usually more useful if you already have a strong SAT score.
SAT-II Old SAT Subject Tests. These were discontinued by the College Board and should not be treated as the current FAST route.
Selected You cleared merit for that campus and program according to FAST's result chart.
Eligible You may be eligible for a lower preference or alternate option, depending on your preference order and available seats.
Denied You did not clear the merit requirement for that campus and program.
IBCC Equivalence The official conversion required for O/A-Level or foreign-board academic records before they can be used in local admissions.
Aggregate Your combined admission score, usually calculated using entry test and academic record weightage.
Closing Merit The last accepted aggregate for a program and campus. FAST does not always publish this directly, so many numbers online are unofficial estimates.

Academic Terms

These terms appear during classes, quizzes, exams, result discussions, registration, and academic-warning situations.

Term Meaning
Ragra Urdu slang for grind or pressure. At FAST, it means the constant academic workload of quizzes, assignments, labs, sessionals, and finals.
Sessional 1 The first mid-semester exam.
Sessional 2 The second mid-semester exam.
Absolutes FAST's internal way students talk about raw marks. In hard courses, the pass/fail margin can sometimes be just a few absolutes.
SGPA Semester Grade Point Average. It measures your performance in one semester only.
CGPA Cumulative Grade Point Average. It measures your overall performance across all completed semesters.
F/A Fail due to attendance shortage. It usually happens when attendance falls below the minimum requirement for final exam eligibility.
I Grade Incomplete grade. It is temporary and must be completed according to the university's rules.
W Grade Withdrawn course. It usually does not count in GPA, but the fee is generally not refunded.
Dean's List Semester honour for strong SGPA, usually 3.50 to 3.99 with regular course load.
Rector's List Semester honour for 4.00 SGPA with regular course load.
FYP-I First semester of the Final Year Project.
FYP-II Second semester of the Final Year Project.
Warning Count The count that increases when your CGPA remains below the minimum required level. If it reaches 3, admission can be closed.
Course Repeat Taking a course again after failing it or to improve the grade. Usually, the most recent grade counts toward CGPA.
Add / Drop Changing registered courses early in the semester, usually within the official add/drop window and with approval.

Financial Terms

These terms matter when you are calculating FAST affordability, applying for aid, or comparing scholarships.

Term Meaning
FA Financial Assistance. At FAST, this is usually need-based and commonly structured as Qarz-e-Hasana.
Qarz-e-Hasana An interest-free loan. It reduces fee pressure during study but usually has to be repaid after graduation or employment.
Honhaar A government scholarship channel that students may apply for if eligible.
PEEF Punjab Educational Endowment Fund, a Punjab-government scholarship channel for eligible students.
OSAF A university-linked support channel that may be announced through FAST communication.
FANA A need-based support channel in FAST's aid ecosystem.
Ihsan Trust An external charitable trust that may support eligible students through its own process and rules.
Security Deposit A refundable amount paid at admission, usually returned after leaving university and clearing dues.
Admission Fee A one-time admission or processing fee. It is usually non-refundable.
Per-Credit-Hour Fee Fee calculated by course credit hours. It matters for repeats, summer courses, and extra courses.
Refund Window The limited time period in which you can withdraw and recover part of your tuition fee.

Related page: Fee Structure

Campus Life Terms

These terms are used when students talk about FAST culture, social life, teachers, and university experience.

Commuter Culture

A campus environment where many students come for classes and leave after their academic work instead of staying on campus all day.

Societies

Student groups that organize events, competitions, social activities, and university-level projects.

Teacher Lottery

Student slang for the way your course experience can change depending on which teacher your section gets.

Viral Gender Rules Post

A satirical post that exaggerated FAST gender-interaction rules. It is not actual university policy.

Senior Network

Connections with older students who can guide you about teachers, resources, internships, and course planning.

Social Capital

Your reputation and relationships in university. It helps with groups, societies, referrals, and academic support.

Career & Future Terms

These terms appear when students discuss internships, local jobs, going abroad, specialisations, and long-term career planning.

Term Meaning
FAST Tag The local reputation advantage FAST has in Pakistan's software and tech job market.
Referral When someone inside a company forwards or recommends your profile for a role.
Portfolio Your visible proof of work, usually GitHub projects, deployed apps, case studies, or technical writing.
GitHub A platform where developers store and share code. A clean GitHub helps show project work to recruiters.
Leetcode A coding-practice platform used for DSA and interview preparation. It is most useful after learning DSA basics.
FAANG Old shorthand for major global big-tech companies. Direct hiring is rare but possible for exceptional profiles.
Cybersecurity A specialised field focused on protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from attacks.
Erasmus Mundus A competitive European joint master's route that may include scholarships for selected students.
Fulbright A highly competitive funded graduate-study route for the United States.
Blocked Account A financial-proof account commonly discussed for Germany. It is usually living-expense proof, not tuition.
Skilled Migration An immigration route based on factors like age, education, English score, work experience, and occupation demand.

Most Confusing FAST Terms

These are the terms students usually misunderstand the most in their first year.

Ragra

It does not mean one hard exam. It means constant weekly academic pressure.

FA

It usually means repayable Financial Assistance, not automatically a free scholarship.

Eligible

It does not always mean selected for your first choice. It can mean a lower preference or alternate option.

F/A

It is not the same as a normal F. It means fail because of attendance shortage.

Absolutes

These are raw marks students talk about, not a separate official grade scale.

Warning Count

This is serious. If it reaches 3, admission can be closed.

Quick Summary

The most important FAST terms to understand early are NU Test, aggregate, Ragra, sessionals, absolutes, SGPA, CGPA, F/A, warning count, FA, Qarz-e-Hasana, and course repeat.

Once you understand these terms, most FAST discussions become easier to follow. Admissions, academics, finances, campus life, and career planning all become less confusing when the vocabulary is clear.
