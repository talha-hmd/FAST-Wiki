FAST Glossary — Ragra, Sessionals, Absolutes, FA & More

Navigating campus conversations at FAST-NUCES can feel like learning a completely different language. This official lexicon breaks down the essential admission codes, academic grading statuses, and local financial terminology used across our campuses.

Admissions Terminology

These structural terms dictate your eligibility metrics, application paths, and entrance options during the initial enrollment cycle.

Term Definition & Field Context
NU Test FAST's proprietary computer-based entrance exam. This is the heavily recommended default admission stream due to its massive seat allocation.
NAT National Aptitude Test conducted by NTS. It features a tiny, highly restricted seat pool at FAST, making admission thresholds volatile and risky.
SAT-I The standard Scholastic Assessment Test reasoning exam managed by the College Board, evaluated on a dedicated international merit stream.
SAT-II Legacy SAT Subject Tests permanently discontinued by the College Board in 2021. Any mentions on local campus sites are completely obsolete.
Selected / Eligible / Denied The three outcomes on the central merit chart. 'Selected' indicates straight admission; 'Eligible' means you qualify for lower preferences or waiting list pools; 'Denied' means you fell below the cutoff.
IBCC Equivalence The mandatory local certificate conversion required for O/A-Level or foreign-board transcripts before submitting to the university registrar.

Academic & Grading Systems

These terms define the daily pace, examinations, and structural failsafes tracking your academic standing throughout each semester.

Term Definition & Field Context
Ragra The classic campus slang for "the absolute grind." It refers to the intense, non-stop weekly pressure of quizzes, coding labs, and back-to-back assignments.
Sessional 1 & 2 The two standardized mid-semester examinations scheduled in the 6th and 11th weeks of the academic period.
Absolutes Internal student slang for unweighted raw marks out of 100. In notoriously difficult courses, passing or failing often hinges on just 2 to 3 absolute marks.
SGPA & CGPA Semester Grade Point Average (performance in a single term) versus Cumulative Grade Point Average (overall running academic average).
F/A An automatic failing grade forced onto a student's transcript when their attendance drops below the mandatory 80% undergraduate cutoff.
I & W 'Incomplete' and 'Withdrawn' markers. These serve as administrative placeholders on your card and carry zero point weight in your CGPA calculations.
Dean's & Rector's List High-honor distinctions awarded for securing a semester SGPA of 3.50–3.99 (Dean's) or a perfect 4.00 (Rector's) under full credit loads.
FYP-I & FYP-II Final Year Project parts one and two, spanning across your 7th and 8th semesters respectively as your core graduation capstone.
Warning Count The critical running count tracking GPA deficits. Falling below a 2.00 cumulative score increments this count; hitting three warnings triggers permanent academic dismissal.

Financial & Scholarship Structures

Review these clear fiscal definitions to manage your ledger entries, aid distribution, and account freezing policies safely.

Term Definition & Field Context
FA (Financial Assistance) The university's need-based aid portal. This is structured almost exclusively as a repayable credit line, not a gift.
Qarz-e-Hasana An interest-free loan protocol underpinning FAST's internal FA system. It defers your tuition payments but must be fully paid back within 4 years of graduating.
Honhaar / PEEF / OSAF / FANA The core provincial government, trust, and institutional scholarship channels that supply non-repayable grants to qualified students.
Semester Freeze Formally pausing your degree track for a semester. This requires a small administrative holding fee, and HEC guidelines state the first two semesters of a BS program cannot be frozen.

Summary of Critical Terms

If you are a newcomer on campus, focus heavily on mastering three metrics immediately: tracking your Absolutes across sessionals to secure your base grades, watching your attendance to avoid an F/A status, and protecting your CGPA above the 2.00 threshold to keep your academic warning count completely clean.
