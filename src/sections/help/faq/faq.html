Help & FAQs

FAST Admissions & Academics — Frequently Asked Questions

This page answers the most common FAST-NUCES questions quickly. For deeper explanations, each answer points you toward the relevant detailed section of FAST Wiki.

Should I take the NU Test or the NTS NAT for FAST?

For most students, take the NU Test. FAST's largest admission pool is usually the NU Test stream, so it is the safest and most practical option for the average applicant.

NAT may look easier as a test, but the reserved seat pool is much smaller. That means the required score can become extremely high and unpredictable.

Best answer: Default to the NU Test unless you already have a near-perfect NAT score.

Does FAST still accept SAT Subject Tests?

No. SAT Subject Tests, also called SAT-II, were discontinued by the College Board. FAST's SAT route is based on SAT-I and usually runs on a separate merit list.

Some old campus pages or screenshots may still mention SAT-II. Treat those as outdated unless the central FAST admissions page confirms them.

Best answer: Use the central FAST admissions portal for SAT rules, not old screenshots.

Read more: SAT Stream

What is the minimum attendance required to sit finals at FAST?

For undergraduate students, the minimum attendance requirement is usually 80%. For graduate or postgraduate students, it is usually 75%.

If your attendance falls below the required limit, you may become ineligible to sit the final exam and receive an F/A grade in that course.

Undergraduate

Usually 80% minimum attendance.

Graduate

Usually 75% minimum attendance.

Below Limit

Final exam may be blocked and F/A may be awarded.

What CGPA do I need to avoid academic warning?

The usual minimum CGPA is 2.00 for BS/BBA, 2.50 for MS/MBA, and 3.00 for PhD. If your CGPA falls below the required minimum, you may receive an academic warning.

If your CGPA remains below the minimum after another semester, your warning count increases. When warning count reaches 3, admission can be closed.

BS / BBA

Minimum CGPA is usually 2.00.

MS / MBA

Minimum CGPA is usually 2.50.

PhD

Minimum CGPA is usually 3.00.

What GPA do I need to go abroad for a funded master's?

A CGPA below 3.0 can make funded master's options difficult because many programs use GPA as an early filter. A 3.3+ CGPA keeps more options open, while 3.5+ usually improves scholarship chances.

At FAST, reaching those numbers can require more discipline because grading is stricter than many other universities. That does not make abroad impossible, but it means you need to protect CGPA from the first year.

Best answer: Aim for 3.3+ minimum if abroad is serious, and 3.5+ if you want stronger scholarship chances.

Read more: Going Abroad

Is FAST Financial Assistance a grant or a loan?

FAST Financial Assistance is usually Qarz-e-Hasana, which means an interest-free loan. It helps reduce the immediate fee burden during your degree, but it usually has to be repaid after graduation or after getting a job.

This means FA can make FAST accessible, but it does not always make FAST cheap. Before accepting, calculate the total amount you may owe after 8 semesters.

Best answer: Treat FAST FA as repayable assistance unless the university clearly confirms otherwise in writing.

Read more: Financial Assistance

Can I afford FAST if my family income is around Rs. 80,000 per month?

It depends on the aid package, city, hostel requirement, and whether your family has backup savings. If FAST requires Financial Assistance plus external borrowing, the decision becomes risky.

Apply to every possible aid channel: FAST FA, Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA, Ihsan Trust, and any merit-based support you qualify for. At the same time, keep a backup university whose fee your family can manage without heavy debt.

Best answer: FAST is worth considering if aid makes it manageable. If it needs extra loans on top of FA, compare cheaper universities very seriously.

Is the viral “gender rules” post about FAST real?

No. The viral post about extreme gender-interaction rules is satire. FAST is a normal co-educational Pakistani university with normal cultural boundaries and basic expectations of respectful behavior.

Students interact through classes, labs, group projects, societies, events, and academic work. The only real rule is to behave maturely and respectfully.

Best answer: The meme is fake. The real campus environment is normal.

Is BS Computer Science better than BS Cybersecurity if I am unsure?

Generally, yes. BS Computer Science is broader and keeps more paths open: software engineering, AI, data science, web development, app development, systems, cloud, and cybersecurity.

Cybersecurity can be a good path if you have confirmed interest in security, networks, systems, ethical hacking, and investigation. But if you are choosing it only because it sounds trendy, CS is usually safer.

Best answer: When uncertain, choose CS. Specialise later through projects, electives, certifications, and internships.

How long do I have to complete my BS at FAST?

For BS/BBA programs, the usual maximum time is 6 years, with a possible extension of one more year through approval. Extensions are usually granted only for genuine unavoidable circumstances.

This limit matters if you freeze semesters, fail courses repeatedly, transfer credits, or fall behind your degree plan.

Best answer: Plan for 4 years, but know that the usual maximum BS/BBA window is 6 years, extendable by approval.

Read more: Academic Failsafes

Is FAST good for local software jobs?

Yes. FAST has one of the strongest local computing brands in Pakistan. The FAST tag can help with CV shortlisting, employer trust, alumni referrals, and local tech-company perception.

But the tag is not magic. You still need real skills, projects, internships, communication, GitHub, and interview preparation.

Best answer: FAST gives local leverage. Skills decide how well you use that leverage.

Read more: Local Jobs

Can a bad first year at FAST be recovered?

Yes, but only if you change your method. Later semesters carry enough credit weight to improve CGPA, but repeating the same habits that caused the damage will not fix the problem.

Focus on repeating weak foundational courses properly, collecting quiz and assignment marks early, reducing overload, and building a consistent weekly routine.

Best answer: A bad first year is recoverable. A broken study system repeated for four years is not.

Read more: Study Strategy

When should I start Leetcode?

Start Leetcode after you have studied Data Structures and Algorithms properly. Before DSA, focus on programming fundamentals, loops, arrays, functions, recursion basics, OOP, and general problem solving.

Starting Leetcode too early can be frustrating because you may be trying to solve problems without understanding the tools behind them.

Best answer: Learn DSA first, then start Leetcode with patterns and consistency.

Read more: Study Strategy

Is FAST worth it?

FAST is worth it for many students, especially those targeting local software jobs in Pakistan and families who can manage the fee without dangerous borrowing.

FAST becomes less obvious if your main goal is a funded master's abroad, your family cannot afford the fee safely, or you already have a strong cheaper alternative.

Best answer: FAST is strong, but the right choice depends on your goal, budget, backup options, and ability to handle academic pressure.

Read more: University Comparisons

Quick Summary

Most FAST questions come down to the same few decisions: choose the NU Test unless you have a strong alternative stream, protect attendance, protect CGPA, understand FA before accepting it, and choose CS if you are unsure about specialisation.

For local jobs, FAST is very strong. For abroad, GPA and profile planning matter more. For affordability, compare the full four-year cost before committing.
