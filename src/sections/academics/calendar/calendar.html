FAST Academic Year, Semesters & the Summer Term

FAST follows a semester-based academic system. If you understand how the academic year, credit hours, regular semesters, and summer term work, it becomes easier to plan your workload, repeats, GPA recovery, and degree timeline.

The Academic Year

The FAST academic year usually starts around August or September and ends around May or June of the next year. It is divided into two regular semesters: Fall and Spring.

Fall Semester

Usually starts around August or September and continues until December or January.

Spring Semester

Usually starts around January and continues until May or June.

Summer Term

Optional and shorter. Mostly used for repeats, improvement, or special allowed courses.

Important: Exact dates can change every year, so always check the official academic calendar shared by your campus.

Regular Semester Structure

A regular semester at FAST is not just classes and then finals. It has teaching weeks, a makeup week, and final examinations.

1

Teaching weeks: Around 16 weeks of lectures, labs, quizzes, assignments, and sessionals.

2

Makeup week: A short buffer period for missed classes, makeup lectures, or pending academic work.

3

Final exams: Around 2 weeks of final examinations at the end of the semester.

This structure is why FAST feels continuous. Your performance is not decided only in finals. The semester keeps moving through quizzes, assignments, labs, sessionals, projects, and then final exams.

Credit Hours and Course Load

Credit hours are used to measure the weight of a course. They affect your timetable, workload, semester fee, and GPA calculation.

Theory Course

1 credit hour usually means 1 hour of lecture per week.

Lab Course

1 credit hour usually means 3 hours of lab work per week.

3 + 1 Course

Means 3 hours of theory lectures plus 3 hours of lab work per week.

Your normal course load is usually based on your program study plan. In most semesters, FAST expects students to register for the prescribed courses unless they are repeating, under warning, or following a special approved plan.

The Summer Semester

The summer semester is optional and shorter than a regular semester. It usually lasts around 8 to 9 weeks, and it is offered at the discretion of the campus administration.

Because summer is shorter, the weekly contact hours are usually doubled. This means the same course content is compressed into a smaller time period, so summer courses can feel intense.

Simple meaning: Summer is not a chill semester. It is shorter, faster, and usually used to repeat or recover courses.

Important Summer Rules

FAST's summer term has special rules. Do not assume it works exactly like Fall or Spring.

  • Summer is optional and usually 8 to 9 weeks long.
  • Contact hours per week are doubled because the course is compressed.
  • Research and FYP courses are not usually offered in summer.
  • You generally cannot register for a brand-new course in summer.
  • You may repeat a limited number of courses, usually maximum 2 courses with labs.
  • Summer courses usually cannot be dropped once registered.
  • Fee is usually not refunded or carried forward for dropped summer courses.
  • Financial assistance, scholarship, or fee concession usually does not apply in summer.
  • A summer course may only run if enough students register.

Why Summer Planning Matters

Summer can be useful if you failed a course, want to improve a weak area, or need to fix your academic flow. But because it is compressed, you should not overload yourself without thinking.

For example, repeating a foundational course like Programming Fundamentals or OOP in summer may help only if you actually rebuild the concepts. If you treat summer as a shortcut, the next semester can become harder.

Good Use

Repeating a weak foundational course and properly rebuilding concepts.

Bad Use

Taking summer casually and expecting compressed courses to be easier.

Best Mindset

Use summer for recovery, not escape. The pace is faster than regular semesters.

Quick Summary

FAST has two regular semesters: Fall and Spring. A regular semester usually includes 16 teaching weeks, a makeup week, and final exams. Credit hours decide course weight, workload, and GPA impact.

The summer semester is optional, shorter, and faster. It can be useful for repeats or recovery, but it has strict rules, limited course options, and no room for casual planning.
