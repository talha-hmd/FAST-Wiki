Attendance, Missed Exams & Academic Integrity at FAST

Some FAST rules can affect your grade even if you understand the course. Attendance shortage, missed assessments, and academic integrity cases are serious because they can directly lead to zero marks, F/A, or even disciplinary action.

Attendance Requirement

FAST expects students to maintain full attendance. In practice, a limited absence margin may be condoned for genuine reasons such as illness, but you should not treat this as free skipping.

For undergraduate students, the minimum attendance required to sit in final exams is usually 80%. For graduate or postgraduate students, it is usually 75%.

Undergraduate

Minimum attendance to sit finals is usually 80%.

Graduate

Minimum attendance to sit finals is usually 75%.

Below Limit

You become ineligible for the final exam and may receive F/A in that course.

Important: F/A means fail due to attendance shortage. It can ruin a course even if your quiz, assignment, or sessional performance was decent.

What Happens If You Miss a Quiz or Assignment?

Missed quizzes and assignments are usually strict. If you miss a quiz or assignment, the normal result is zero marks. There is generally no retake system for routine quizzes or assignments.

This is why small assessments matter at FAST. You may think one quiz is not important, but multiple missed quizzes can damage your semester marks before finals even start.

  • Missed quiz usually means zero marks.
  • Missed assignment usually means zero marks.
  • Retakes are generally not given for normal quizzes or assignments.
  • Repeated small zeros can badly affect your final grade.
Simple rule: Do not casually skip small assessments. At FAST, small marks often decide whether your final grade survives.

What Happens If You Miss a Midterm or Final?

Midterms and final exams are treated more seriously than quizzes. A retake may be possible, but only in specific serious cases such as illness, serious accident, or death in the immediate family.

The request is usually examined by a faculty committee. This means you need proper documentation and a genuine reason. A casual excuse will not normally be accepted.

1

You miss a midterm or final due to a serious reason.

2

You submit an official request with proof or supporting documents.

3

A faculty committee reviews the case.

4

If approved, the retake is scheduled according to university rules.

5

For final exam retakes, the grade must be finalized before the next semester begins.

Reminder: If a final exam retake is approved, a retake exam fee may be required before the exam.

Academic Dishonesty

Academic dishonesty includes cheating, using unfair means, copying, helping someone cheat, submitting copied work, or violating assessment rules. FAST treats these cases seriously.

The minimum punishment for unfair means in an assessment can be zero marks in that assessment. If the matter is escalated to a disciplinary committee, the punishment can become much more serious.

Minimum Penalty

Zero marks in the assessment where unfair means were used.

Department Level

If referred to the department committee, the maximum can be F in that course.

Campus Level

If escalated further, consequences can include F in all courses, suspension, or expulsion.

Do not risk it: Copying one assignment or using unfair means in one exam can create consequences far bigger than the marks you were trying to save.

The Reality: Contesting an Unfair Accusation

Sometimes students feel they have been accused unfairly. If that happens, panic and anger usually make things worse. The better approach is to stay calm, create a paper trail, and escalate step by step.

Start by speaking calmly to the teacher and understanding the exact accusation. Ask whether it is a warning, partial deduction, full cancellation, or a formal disciplinary case.

1

Talk to the teacher calmly: Understand what exactly has been alleged.

2

Write to the HOD if needed: Submit a respectful written application explaining your case.

3

Ask for evidence: If the case involves an exam hall, request CCTV footage or relevant proof.

4

Stay professional: Do not fight publicly, post accusations online, or make the case emotional.

Practical advice: Keep communication respectful and written where possible. A calm paper trail is stronger than emotional arguments.

How to Protect Yourself

The best protection is simple: track attendance, submit work on time, keep proof of submissions, and avoid anything that can look suspicious during assessments.

  • Check your attendance regularly on the portal.
  • Do not assume teachers will “adjust” attendance later.
  • Submit assignments before the deadline, not at the last second.
  • Keep screenshots or email proof of important submissions.
  • Do not share your code or assignment files casually.
  • Do not sit in a way that creates suspicion during exams.
  • Keep medical documents if illness affects attendance or exams.
  • For serious cases, communicate through proper written channels.

Quick Summary

Attendance, missed exams, and academic integrity rules can directly affect your grade at FAST. Undergraduate students usually need at least 80% attendance to sit finals. Falling below the limit can lead to F/A.

Missed quizzes or assignments usually mean zero marks. Midterm and final retakes are only for serious cases with approval. Academic dishonesty can lead from zero marks to F grades, suspension, or worse depending on escalation.
