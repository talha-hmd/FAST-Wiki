Attendance, Missed Exams & Academic Integrity at FAST

Some FAST rules can affect your grade even if you understand the course. Attendance shortage, missed assessments, and academic integrity cases are serious because they can directly lead to zero marks, F/A, or even disciplinary action.

Attendance Requirement

FAST mandates a baseline 100% attendance policy. The university allows absences of up to 20% of total lectures to be condoned strictly for verified reasons, such as medical emergencies or severe illness. This condonation allowance is never automatic—the candidate must formally submit a documented reason for university administration approval.

Falling entirely below the structural minimum limits renders you completely ineligible to sit for the end-semester evaluation, resulting in an automatic FA grade (Fail due to Attendance shortage) for that course block.

Undergraduate

Absolute minimum attendance required to sit final exams is strictly 80%.

Graduate

Absolute minimum attendance required to sit final exams is strictly 75%.

Below Limit

Instant loss of examination eligibility, wiping out course progress with an unappealable F/A status flag.

Important: F/A means fail due to attendance shortage. It can ruin a course even if your quiz, assignment, or sessional performance was decent.

What Happens If You Miss a Quiz or Assignment?

Routine localized assessments feature highly rigid system constraints. If you miss a quiz or an assignment deadline, you receive zero marks for that specific component.

FAST maintains an absolute no-retake provision for missing routine quizzes or assignments. Missing these small markers iteratively creates severe downstream damage, slashing your internal sessional aggregate before the final testing window opens.

  • Missing any quiz or assignment results in an immediate zero mark.
  • No retake or makeups exist for routine ongoing tasks.
  • Sessional aggregates depend on stacking these small assessment parameters.
  • Repeated small zeros can badly affect your final grade.

What Happens If You Miss a Midterm or Final Exam?

Major midterms and terminal final examinations carry strict institutional procedures. A makeup examination can only be unlocked for extreme situations: documented personal illness, severe accidents, or a death in the immediate family. All appeals are reviewed by a specialized Faculty Committee.

1

You miss a midterm or final exam block due to a verified, serious crisis.

2

You submit a formal application accompanied by authoritative proof or medical documentation.

3

The centralized Faculty Committee evaluates the legitimacy and corroborating data of the request.

4

If approved, the final exam retake must be held and graded no later than one week before the subsequent semester launches.

5

The candidate completes the required retake exam fee payment clearing before the makeup test is administered.

Official Academic Dishonesty Regulations

Academic dishonesty incorporates plagiarism, cheating during examinations, copying code fragments, or submitting shared assets. FAST enforces a zero-tolerance baseline across all assessment variants:

Minimum Penalty

An automatic, non-negotiable zero marks in the specific quiz, test, midterm, or assignment where unfair means were deployed.

Department Level

If referred by the instructor to the Department Disciplinary Committee, the maximum penalty escalates to an immediate F grade in the course.

Campus Level

If referred by the HOD to the Campus Disciplinary Committee, the penalty escalates to an F grade in all courses that semester, alongside suspension or permanent expulsion tracks.

The Reality: Contesting an Unfair Accusation

If you face an academic dishonesty allegation without solid proof, avoid panic or hostile public confrontations. You should navigate the situation using a highly disciplined, graduated escalation model:

1

Calm Teacher Consultation: Engage in a professional dialogue to confirm whether the issue is handled as an informal deduction or a formal disciplinary referral.

2

Formal HOD Application: If the teacher remains unyielding without cause, file a respectful written application to the HOD documenting your standalone code logic or exam actions.

3

Request CCTV Verification: For exam hall disputes, explicitly request the committee to review the official hall CCTV footage to verify your posture and visibility tracks.

4

Pragmatic Execution: Disciplinary committees cannot assign an F grade without corroborating evidence. If you must retake a course with that instructor, maintain a low profile and secure high marks to let your work speak for itself.

How to Protect Yourself

The best protection is simple: track attendance, submit work on time, keep proof of submissions, and avoid anything that can look suspicious during assessments.

  • Check your attendance regularly on the portal.
  • Do not assume teachers will “adjust” attendance later.
  • Submit assignments before the deadline, not at the last second.
  • Keep screenshots or email proof of important submissions.
  • Do not share your code or assignment files casually.
  • Do not sit in a way that creates suspicion during exams.
  • Keep medical documents if illness affects attendance or exams.
  • For serious cases, communicate through proper written channels.

Quick Summary

FAST enforces an unyielding 100% attendance expectation, requiring a hard floor of 80% for undergraduates to prevent an automatic FA course failure grade. Missing routine assignments or quizzes scores an unrecoverable zero.

Approved sessional makeups are strictly limited to severe medical emergencies. Unfair means cases trigger immediate zeros, cascading up to departmental course failures or total semester exclusions depending on whether the Department or Campus Disciplinary Committee executes the tracking query.
