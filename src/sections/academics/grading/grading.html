FAST Grading — Letter Grades, GPA, CGPA & the Dean's List

FAST's grading system can feel confusing at first because your final grade is not based only on the final exam. Quizzes, assignments, sessionals, labs, projects, and finals all contribute to your result. This page explains the grade scale, SGPA, CGPA, repeats, and honours lists in simple terms.

How Courses Are Assessed

At the start of a course, the instructor usually shares a course outline. This outline explains the course content, assessment method, grading criteria, and the weight of different components.

A course may include quizzes, assignments, lab work, projects, case studies, sessionals, and a final exam. In many courses, semester work and the final exam both matter heavily.

Semester Work

Quizzes, assignments, labs, projects, presentations, and sessionals collected during the semester.

Final Exam

The end-semester exam that usually carries a major part of the course weight.

Course Outline

The document that tells you how your course will be assessed.

Important: Do not wait for finals to start caring. At FAST, continuous assessment can decide your grade long before the final exam.

Letter Grades and Grade Points

FAST uses letter grades, and each letter grade has a grade point value. These grade points are used to calculate your SGPA and CGPA.

Letter Grade Grade Points
A+ 4.00
A 4.00
A- 3.67
B+ 3.33
B 3.00
B- 2.67
C+ 2.33
C 2.00
C- 1.67
D+ 1.33
D 1.00
F 0.00

For undergraduate students, this scale is the main one you will usually deal with. Graduate and PhD programs may have slightly different grade rules, so those should be checked separately from the official academic regulations.

Special Grades

Not every grade is a normal letter grade like A, B, C, D, or F. FAST also uses special grades for specific academic situations.

Grade Meaning GPA Impact
F/A Fail due to attendance shortage Counts as 0.00
I Incomplete Not counted immediately, but can convert to F if requirements are not completed
W Withdrawn Not counted in GPA
CN Continue research Used only in specific thesis/research cases
S / U Satisfactory / Unsatisfactory Used for specific thesis work, not regular GPA calculation
Watch out: F/A is dangerous because it usually means you failed because of attendance shortage, even if you understood the course.

SGPA vs CGPA

SGPA and CGPA are both averages, but they are not the same thing.

SGPA

Semester Grade Point Average. It shows your performance in one semester only.

CGPA

Cumulative Grade Point Average. It shows your overall performance across all semesters.

Credit Weight

Courses with more credit hours affect GPA more than courses with fewer credit hours.

GPA is weighted by credit hours. This means a 4-credit course affects your GPA more than a 1-credit lab or smaller course. So when planning GPA recovery, focus more on high-credit courses.

How Repeats Affect CGPA

If you repeat a course, FAST usually counts the most recent grade in CGPA calculation. This can help if you improve your grade, but it can also hurt if your repeated attempt is worse.

The important part is that repeat courses should not be treated casually. If you repeat a course, your goal should be to properly rebuild the concepts and improve the grade.

Repeat rule: Only the most recent grade points are used for CGPA calculation, even if the new grade is lower than the older one.

Dean's List and Rector's List

FAST has honours lists for strong semester performance. These are based on SGPA, not CGPA, and usually require students to complete the regular course load.

Dean's List

Usually awarded for SGPA between 3.50 and 3.99 with regular course load.

Rector's List

Usually awarded for SGPA 4.00 with regular course load.

Recognition

Names may be displayed by the university, and certificates may be issued.

A strong SGPA in one semester can earn recognition even if your overall CGPA is still recovering. This is why one good semester can matter a lot for confidence and academic momentum.

Quick Summary

FAST grades are based on continuous semester work and final exams. Letter grades convert into grade points, and those grade points are used to calculate SGPA and CGPA.

SGPA is for one semester. CGPA is your overall average. Repeats can improve your CGPA, but only the latest attempt counts, so repeat courses carefully. Dean's List and Rector's List reward strong semester performance.
