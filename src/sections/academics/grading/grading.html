FAST Grading — Letter Grades, GPA, CGPA & the Dean's List

FAST's grading system can feel confusing at first because your final grade is not based only on the final exam. Quizzes, assignments, sessionals, labs, projects, and finals all contribute to your result. This page explains the grade scale, SGPA, CGPA, repeats, and honours lists in simple terms.

How Courses Are Assessed

Performance is assessed continuously throughout the semester. A comprehensive course outline detailing foundational contents, sequential modules, and explicit grading criteria is shared officially at the start of each course.

Semester Work

Accounts for 50% of the weight toward your final letter grade, evaluated through midterms, localized assignments, quizzes, and case studies.

Final Examination

Accounts for the remaining 50% of the course weight, functioning as a centralized terminal test at the end of the academic cycle.

Evaluation Outline

The binding document provided by the instructor at day one that ensures structural clarity for all grading weight distributions.

Important: Do not wait for finals to start caring. At FAST, continuous assessment can decide your grade long before the final exam.

SGPA vs CGPA Mechanics

Averages are heavily dependent on course load weights. Both configurations calculate weighted averages where individual course credit hours function as the multiplier:

  • SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average): Calculated by taking the sum of your earned grade points multiplied by the course credit hours, divided by the total credit hours registered during that specific semester block.
  • CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average): Reflects the overall cumulative weighted performance across all academic courses taken throughout your entire timeline at the university.

How Repeats Affect CGPA

When a student repeats an instruction block for improvement or remedial recovery, FAST applies a strict chronological replacement rule inside its central tracking system:

The Replacement Mandate: Only the most recent grade points are used toward the cumulative CGPA calculation. This is a double-edged sword—the system records your latest score even if your repeated attempt yields a lower grade than the previous registration block.

Official Academic Honours Lists

FAST publicly recognizes superior academic semester outputs. To be eligible for either honours track, you must complete your semester while carrying a full, regular course load study plan:

Dean's List of Honors

Requires an SGPA between 3.50 and 3.99. Earns certificate recognition and placement on official campus notice panels and the web directory.

Rector's List of Honours

Requires a perfect semester SGPA of 4.00. Grants a specialized honors certificate alongside public registry placement.

Strategic Impact

Because these lists check SGPA in isolation, a single high-velocity semester can unlock an honors profile even while your master CGPA is actively recovering.

Quick Summary

FAST evaluates courses using a 50/50 breakdown between semester work and terminal exams. Points map to letter grades across distinct undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral rules, supplemented by system flags like FA or I.

Averages use a credit-weighted calculation model. When repeating a course, the most recent attempt entirely replaces earlier points in your CGPA calculation, and superior semester SGPAs are rewarded via the regular course load Dean's and Rector's lists.
