FAST NUCES Course Registration 2026: Add, Drop & Withdrawal Rules

Course registration decides exactly what you study each term. At FAST, this is a major administrative step. Small mistakes with add/drop deadlines, parent signatures, or prerequisite failures can directly delay your graduation timeline and create unexpected fee overheads.

Semester Registration & The Portal Race

During the Fall and Spring enrollment blocks, you must officially register for your subjects online and settle your financial balances. Official semester fees are always due two weeks before the semester starts. If you miss this primary window, you will be hit with a mandatory Rs. 5,000 late registration fee.

The university requires every regular student to enroll in a full, standard course load layout unless they are restricted by an active academic warning or have obtained special permission from the academic office.

Load Structuring: Choose your semester subjects in alignment with your program's official roadmap, keeping credit hour limits in mind if you are on probation.

Freshman Enrollment: First-semester university freshmen are registered directly by the Academic Office to make sure their baseline schedules are locked down seamlessly.

4

Senior Strategy — The Portal Race Warning: Seniors emphasize that course registration at FAST is a high-speed race. Subject slots—especially sections taught by popular, high-scoring instructors—fill up completely within a matter of seconds. Do not log in casually. Have your specific course codes written down on a notepad, log into your portal account ten minutes early, and hit submit the exact second the registration window opens.

Adding or Dropping a Course

The add/drop process provides a brief administrative window at the very start of the semester to adjust your weekly schedule. While it allows you to correct any registration mistakes, it follows strict university limits:

Two-Week Wall

All subject modifications, section swaps, or elective updates must be fully finalized within the first two weeks of the semester.

HOD Authorization

Submitting an add/drop application requires mandatory written approval from your HOD (Head of Department). It is never a simple self-service option.

First-Semester Floor

You are completely barred from dropping or swapping any subjects during your first semester. Your starting freshman syllabus is entirely non-negotiable.

Withdrawing from a Course

Withdrawing is a completely separate process that takes place later in the semester. While dropping a course completely removes it from your profile during the first two weeks, a formal withdrawal allows you to step away from a class mid-semester if your sessional marks are dangerously low, protecting your CGPA from a devastating F grade.

However, mid-term course withdrawals are governed by strict regulations:

  • No First-Semester Exceptions: Course withdrawals are entirely disallowed for first-semester freshmen.
  • Parental Endorsement: Every single withdrawal application must be physically signed and authorized by your parent or guardian.
  • The Hard Deadline: All signed applications must be cleared and submitted to the HOD before the final day of formal teaching classes.
  • Processing Fees: Withdrawing from a subject after the initial two-week add/drop window closes incurs a mandatory Rs. 5,000 withdrawal processing fee.
  • Theory and Lab Coupling: If a course includes a core laboratory component, you are forced to withdraw from both the theory class and the lab section simultaneously.
  • Zero Refunds: All tuition fees paid for a withdrawn course are completely non-refundable and will not be carried over to future semesters under any condition.

Repeating a Course: Rules & Transcript Impact

The university applies a strict replacement policy to manage academic course repetitions. While it provides a vital lifeline to recover from a rough semester, it leaves a permanent record on your profile:

Mandatory Repeats

You are forced to repeat and clear every failed course. Students on probation must also repeat passed subjects if their grade is too low to sustain a safe 2.0 CGPA baseline.

Grade Replacement

Only your most recent grade points count toward your cumulative CGPA. The system overwrites your older score, even if your new repeat attempt scores a lower grade.

Permanent Notation

An official repeat count marker remains permanently printed on your final university transcript next to every single course re-registration.

The Concept Rebuild Strategy: Despite the permanent notation and the fact that repeating a subject costs you the full out-of-pocket tuition price of Rs. 12,000 per credit hour, repeating a core foundational course (like Programming Fundamentals) immediately in the next semester is a highly proven success strategy. Alumni note that fixing conceptual coding gaps early pays off massively, resulting in a much stronger final CGPA than coasting ahead with a weak, borderline pass.

How to register for FYP at FAST University

Your FYP (Final Year Project) is a high-stakes, two-semester milestone that represents your entire degree's practical conclusion. It must be completed sequentially across FYP-I followed by FYP-II. Registration is strictly controlled by the department based on your credit hour completion records:

Candidate Academic Situation FYP-I Registration Eligibility Required System Approval Bound
All courses successfully passed from semesters 1 to 6 Fully Approved for standard enrollment. Standard automated portal clearance.
Failing / Deficient in exactly one course Allowed to register conditionally. Requires explicit written approval from the HOD.
Failing / Deficient in exactly two courses Allowed to register conditionally. Requires explicit written approval from the Campus Director.
Failing / Deficient in more than two courses Strictly Blocked. You cannot register. No administrative exception available.
Student is under an active Academic Warning Strictly Blocked. Warning flags lock entry. You must clear your probation status first.
Senior Advice — The FYP Partner Strategy: Seniors warn that when forming your Final Year Project group, choosing a partner with a similar GPA and a solid work ethic is far more important than the project idea itself. If your partner is on the edge of academic suspension, a fresh warning flag can drop them from the university roster mid-way through the year, completely ruining the project timeline and graduation dates for both of you. Select your group carefully.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most academic timeline delays happen because students misunderstand deadlines or miscalculate their course limits:

  • Assuming add/drop is flexible: Trying to adjust schedules or change teachers after the two-week portal wall locks down.
  • Confusing drops with withdrawals: Dropping a class early clears it from your record completely, whereas withdrawing later leaves a permanent "W" on your transcript and costs an extra Rs. 5,000 processing fee.
  • Forgetting the repeat score override: Registering to improve a passed C or C- grade without realizing that if you bomb the repeat exam, the new lower score permanently takes over your CGPA.
  • Delaying core prerequisite repeats: Postponing failed gateway subjects like Programming Fundamentals, which eventually blocks you from registering for all advanced senior-year development tracks.

Quick Summary

Semester course registration requires clearing past arrears and settling tuition fees two weeks before classes open to avoid a Rs. 5,000 late fee penalty. The add/drop window locks after two weeks and requires HOD approval, while course withdrawals must be submitted before classes end, incur a Rs. 5,000 processing fee, require parent signatures, and offer zero refunds.

Failed prerequisite subjects must be cleared. Since your latest attempt completely overrides previous grades in your CGPA, repeats require careful planning and cost Rs. 12,000 per credit hour. Finally, registering for FYP-I requires passing your first six semesters, carries a maximum deficiency allowance of two courses with Director approval, and is completely blocked for any student on academic warning probation.
