Course Registration, Add/Drop, Withdrawal & Repeating Courses

Course registration decides what you officially study in a semester. At FAST, this matters because registration, add/drop, withdrawal, repeats, and FYP rules can affect your fee, CGPA, degree plan, and graduation timeline.

Semester Registration

In Fall and Spring semesters, students register for courses and pay the current semester fee. Usually, students are expected to follow the normal course load given in their program study plan.

Before registration, previous dues normally need to be cleared. First-semester students are usually registered through the Academic Office, while continuing students usually register online.

2

Important: Do not delay fee or registration steps casually. Registration problems can affect your semester start and course access.

Adding or Dropping a Course

Add/drop is the short window near the start of the semester where students can request changes in their registered courses. It is useful when a course plan needs correction, but it is not unlimited.

Requests usually need to be made within the first two weeks of the semester, and HOD approval is essential. First-semester students generally cannot drop any course.

Time Limit

Add/drop requests are usually made within the first two weeks of the semester.

Approval Needed

HOD approval is required. It is not just a personal choice.

First Semester

First-semester students generally cannot drop courses.

Simple rule: Add/drop is for early correction, not late escape. Decide quickly and follow the official approval process.

Withdrawing from a Course

Withdrawal is different from dropping. Dropping usually happens early in the semester, while withdrawal happens later when you officially leave a course before the deadline.

Withdrawal is not allowed in the first semester. For other semesters, the application usually needs to be countersigned by a parent or guardian and submitted to the HOD before the last day of classes.

  • Withdrawal is not allowed in the first semester.
  • The application must usually be countersigned by a parent or guardian.
  • The request must be submitted to the HOD before the last day of classes.
  • If a course has both theory and lab, withdrawal is from both theory and lab.
  • Fee is usually not refunded for a withdrawn course.
Watch out: A withdrawn course may not hurt your GPA like an F, but it can still affect your degree plan, course sequence, and fee.

Repeating a Course

Failed courses must be repeated. Students can also repeat passed courses to improve their grade, but repeats should be planned carefully because only the most recent grade is usually counted in CGPA calculation.

This means repeating can help your CGPA if you improve, but it can also hurt if your new grade is lower. A repeat count also appears on the transcript.

Failed Course

You must repeat failed courses.

Grade Improvement

You may repeat a passed course to improve the grade.

Latest Grade Counts

The most recent grade usually counts, even if it is lower.

Repeat carefully: Do not repeat only because you are emotional about a bad grade. Repeat when you have a real plan to improve.

The Smart Way to Use Repeats

Repeating a course can be very useful when the course is foundational. For example, repeating Programming Fundamentals or OOP properly can rebuild weak concepts and make later courses easier.

The goal should not be just to clear the course. The goal should be to fix the reason you struggled the first time.

  • Repeat weak foundational courses early instead of dragging weak concepts forward.
  • Do not repeat too many difficult courses together.
  • Use the repeat to rebuild concepts, not just chase marks.
  • Ask seniors or advisors before repeating a passed course.
  • Remember that the latest attempt affects CGPA.

FYP Registration

Final Year Project is usually completed in two regular semesters: FYP-I followed by FYP-II. You cannot treat it like a normal course that can be added randomly without meeting requirements.

To register for FYP-I, students usually need to have passed the courses from the first six semesters of the study plan. Some deficiency may be allowed with approval, but students with too many deficiencies cannot register.

Situation FYP-I Registration Status
All first six semester courses passed Usually allowed
Deficiency of one course May be allowed by HOD
Deficiency of two courses May be allowed by Director
Deficiency of more than two courses Usually not allowed
Student is on academic warning Usually not allowed for FYP-I
FYP-II rule: FYP-II is allowed only after successful completion of FYP-I.

Common Mistakes Students Make

Most registration problems happen because students delay decisions, misunderstand withdrawal, or repeat courses without understanding the CGPA impact.

  • Thinking add/drop can be done anytime in the semester.
  • Confusing dropping a course with withdrawing from a course.
  • Forgetting that withdrawn course fee is usually not refunded.
  • Repeating a passed course without knowing that the latest grade counts.
  • Ignoring failed foundational courses and then struggling in later semesters.
  • Planning FYP without checking deficiencies and warning status.

Quick Summary

Semester registration is the official process of enrolling in your courses. Add/drop is only available early, usually within the first two weeks, and requires approval. Withdrawal happens later but has stricter conditions and usually does not refund the fee.

Failed courses must be repeated, and passed courses may be repeated for improvement. But only the latest grade usually counts toward CGPA, so repeats should be planned carefully. FYP registration also has strict deficiency and warning-related rules.
