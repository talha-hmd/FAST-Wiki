FAST Academic Failsafes — Repeats, Freeze, Transfers & Reviews

FAST has strict academic rules, but it also has failsafes. These are options that can help when a course goes badly, a semester becomes unmanageable, or your degree plan needs adjustment. The key is to use them early and correctly.

Course Repetition

Course repetition functions as a critical academic recovery tool at FAST. If you fail a course, you must repeat it. You may also repeat a passed course to improve your grade, but this requires deliberate planning.

Under FAST regulations, only the most recent grade earned counts toward your cumulative GPA calculation—even if the repeat attempt results in a lower mark. Furthermore, a permanent repeat count notation is appended to the transcript. Repeat fees are billed dynamically per credit hour; the current official rates can be cross-checked on the university portal at nu.edu.pk/Admissions/FeeStructure.

Failed Course

Failed courses must be repeated.

Latest Grade

Only the most recent attempt counts in your final CGPA calculation.

Transcript Record

A repeat count notation appears permanently on the official transcript.

Best use: Repeat a failed foundational course early and use the repeat to rebuild concepts, not just to clear the grade.

Grade Rechecking and Review

If you receive a suspicious final-exam grade, you have the right to challenge potential grading errors. You must manage this using a strict administrative process:

1

Initial Assessment: Review your sessional components and identify the exact discrepancy in your final marks.

2

Paper Inspection: Request a formal appointment with your instructor to physically view and inspect your final exam script.

3

Formal Escalation: If the instructor refuses paper visibility, escalate the dispute directly to the HOD via a structured, written application.

4

Professional Stature: Marking evaluation can occasionally prove inconsistent; maintaining a polite demeanor backed by a clear paper trail is your most effective recourse.

Semester Freeze Parameters

Under exceptional circumstances or severe personal hardship, students can request an official Semester Freeze from the Campus Director. This mechanism allows you to temporarily step away from your studies without risking programmatic closure, subject to strict institutional constraints:

  • The First-Year Lock: Freezing your first two semesters (for BS candidates) or your very first semester (for MS candidates) is strictly prohibited.
  • Hardship Exemptions: First-year freezes are only unlocked via Competent Authority approval for extreme hardships, specifically: Iddat, maternity/delivery, or a death in your immediate family.
  • The Financial Pivot Point: Executing a freeze costs approximately Rs. 2,000 if finalized before enrolling in any courses. Freezing after formal course enrollment triggers full fee liability for that term.
  • Campus Caps: Both the exact freeze fee and the maximum number of allowable frozen terms are regulated by independent campus administrations. Confirm current metrics directly with your campus Academic Office.

Inter-Campus Transfer Regulations

Migrating between geographic FAST campuses is treated as an exceptional administrative variance. The university will not consider transfer requests based on personal lifestyle preferences or city choices:

Approved Transfer Catalysts Absolute Disqualifying Red Flags
Sudden death of a primary parent or guardian. The student is currently under active disciplinary punishment.
Official government or corporate relocation/posting of a parent to a new city. Active, pending disciplinary inquiries or unresolved behavioral investigations.
Formal marriage of a female student requiring geographic relocation. Outstanding financial balances or unpaid semester dues on the portal.
Requests submitted formally between active semester terms. Transfer attempts initiated mid-semester while classes are already running.

Maximum Time to Complete the Degree

FAST applies strict maximum degree completion limits aligned with Higher Education Commission (HEC) national guidelines. Failing to graduate within these boundaries results in absolute termination of your enrollment link:

Degree Program Official HEC Maximum Duration Boundary
BS / BBA 6 Years maximum (Extendable by 1 additional year via Statutory Bodies' approval).
MBA 3 Years maximum.
MS 4 Years maximum.
PhD 8 Years maximum.

Extensions are only granted for verified, unavoidable hardships (such as documented medical crises or family bereavement). Note that for any student readmitted with credit exemptions, the maximum duration clock starts counting from their original, first admission date, not the re-entry date.

Readmission for Former FAST Students

Former FAST students seeking to resume their education can apply for formal readmission under strict credit-transfer boundaries:

  • Undergraduate Requirements: Completed credits are transferable only if earned within the last 5 years with a minimum grade of C.
  • Graduate Requirements: MS credits are transferable only if earned within the last 5 years with a minimum grade of B- (GPA ≥ 2.67).
  • The CGPA Reset: Prior CGPA metrics do not transfer into your new enrollment profile; your grade point average resets completely.
  • Thesis Block: Prior MS Thesis credits are entirely non-transferable.
  • Warning Restrictions: Students dismissed due to disciplinary actions are barred from re-entry. Candidates whose admission closed due to a 3rd academic warning are permitted readmission exactly once.

External Credit Transfers & Pre-Medical Provisions

Transferring into FAST from an external university requires clearing major competitive and curriculum benchmarks:

Merit Validation

Candidates must sit for and successfully qualify the standard FAST admission test merit cut-off for that specific cycle.

50% Exemption Cap

Total course exemptions cannot exceed 50% of the total credit hours required to finish the FAST degree.

Grade Floors

No credit transfers are allowed for external courses graded below a B- (GPA less than 2.67). External CGPAs and MS Thesis credits never transfer.

Pre-Medical Migration Rule: Pre-medical background students transferring from other institutions must provide authoritative proof of passing an additional, comprehensive Mathematics course alongside its verified syllabus. Final admission remains strictly subject to the technical evaluation of the FAST Admission Committee.

Quick Summary

FAST's academic recovery tools operate under tight administrative limits. Repeating courses replaces your previous CGPA weight but leaves a permanent transcript note, while paper reviews require a polite, written escalation process. Freezing a semester requires taking action before class selection to avoid full fee liabilities, and first-year freezes are restricted to extreme hardships.

Degree completion time frames strictly mirror HEC caps (6 years for BS, 4 years for MS), and external transfers require clearing current entry merit tests. Transferring external courses requires a minimum grade of B-, with exemptions strictly capped at 50% of your total degree plan.
