FAST Academic Failsafes — Repeats, Freeze, Transfers & Reviews

FAST has strict academic rules, but it also has failsafes. These are options that can help when a course goes badly, a semester becomes unmanageable, or your degree plan needs adjustment. The key is to use them early and correctly.

Course Repetition

Course repetition is one of the most important academic recovery tools at FAST. If you fail a course, you must repeat it. You may also repeat a passed course to improve your grade, but this should be done carefully.

The most important rule is that only the most recent grade usually counts toward CGPA. This means a repeat can improve your CGPA if you perform better, but it can also hurt if your new grade is worse.

Failed Course

Failed courses must be repeated.

Latest Grade

The most recent attempt usually counts in CGPA calculation.

Transcript Record

Repeat count can appear on the transcript.

Best use: Repeat a failed foundational course early and use the repeat to rebuild concepts, not just to clear the grade.

Grade Rechecking and Review

If you feel your final exam grade or paper checking has a genuine issue, the first step is to request to view the paper. Do not start with anger or accusations.

If the request is refused or the issue is not resolved, escalate politely to the HOD in writing. A respectful written trail is stronger than verbal complaints.

1

Review your marks and identify the exact issue.

2

Request to view the paper or understand the marking.

3

If needed, write a respectful application to the HOD.

4

Keep communication polite, factual, and documented.

Practical advice: FAST marking can feel inconsistent sometimes, so politeness plus a paper trail is the safest posture.

Semester Freeze

A semester freeze means taking a semester off under exceptional circumstances. It is not a normal break or vacation. Usually, you request the Campus Director for approval and pay a semester freeze fee.

Freezing the first two semesters in BS programs is usually not allowed. For MS students, freezing the first semester is usually not allowed. Special hardship exceptions may exist, but they need proper approval.

Allowed Reason

Exceptional circumstances such as serious hardship or unavoidable personal situations.

Approval Needed

Usually requires a request to the Campus Director.

Fee Applies

A freeze fee is usually payable.

Timing matters: Freezing before enrolling in courses is usually much cleaner. Freezing after enrollment can create full-fee liability, so decide early.

Transfer Between Campuses

Campus transfer is possible only in specific cases. It is not usually allowed just because a student wants a different city or campus environment.

Commonly accepted reasons include death of a parent, posting of a parent to that city, or marriage of a female student. Even then, approval is not automatic.

Situation Transfer Possibility
Death of a parent May be considered
Parent posted to that city May be considered
Marriage of a female student May be considered
Pending disciplinary action Usually not considered
Outstanding dues Usually not considered
During the semester Usually not considered
Simple rule: Do not plan your admission assuming you can easily transfer campuses later.

Maximum Time to Complete the Degree

FAST follows maximum degree completion limits. These limits matter if you fail courses, freeze semesters, transfer credits, or fall behind your study plan.

Program Maximum Time
BS / BBA Usually 6 years, extendable by 1 more year with approval
MBA Usually 3 years
MS Usually 4 years
PhD Usually 8 years

Extensions are usually granted only for genuine unavoidable circumstances, not because a student casually delayed the degree plan.

Readmission for Former FAST Students

Former FAST students may be considered for readmission, but credit transfer depends on program level, time gap, grade, and disciplinary record.

For undergraduate readmission, courses may be transferable if they were completed within the last 5 years with at least C. For graduate readmission, the minimum is usually B-. CGPA itself does not transfer.

  • Credits usually need to be from the last 5 years.
  • Undergraduate credits usually need at least C.
  • Graduate credits usually need at least B-.
  • CGPA from the old admission does not transfer.
  • MS Thesis credits usually do not transfer.
  • A former student on disciplinary warning is usually not readmitted.

Credit Transfer from Another University

Credit transfer from another university is not automatic. The student must usually qualify FAST's merit for that year and program, and the courses must match FAST's curriculum.

Even if courses are accepted, the exemption cannot exceed a certain portion of the degree. Also, CGPA from the previous institution does not transfer.

Merit Required

You still need to qualify through FAST's admission process.

Course Match

FAST checks relevance and compatibility with its own curriculum.

No CGPA Transfer

Previous university CGPA does not become your FAST CGPA.

  • Credit exemption cannot exceed 50% of total degree credit hours.
  • No credit is usually given for a course below B-.
  • MS Thesis credits are usually not transferable.
  • FAST decides compatibility against its own curriculum.

Pre-Medical Transfers

Pre-medical students transferring from another university need special attention because computing programs require mathematics preparation.

Such students usually need to submit proof of passing an additional Mathematics course along with the detailed syllabus. Admission depends on the Admission Committee's assessment.

Important: Pre-medical transfer cases are not automatic. Mathematics proof and syllabus evaluation matter.

Quick Summary

FAST has several academic failsafes: repeats, grade reviews, semester freeze, campus transfer, readmission, and credit transfer. These options can help, but they are controlled by rules and approvals.

The best strategy is to act early. Repeat weak foundational courses properly, keep written proof for grade review, decide semester freeze before enrollment if possible, and never assume transfers or credit exemptions are automatic.
