# FAST-WIKI — Website Content Blueprint

**How this document is structured:** The site is organized into **Macro-Sections** (the top-level navigation). Each macro-section contains **Sub-Sections**, and **every sub-section is its own dedicated webpage** with its own URL, title, and set of **Headings**. Content under each heading is crisp, unambiguous, and complete — enough that an applicant scanning at speed can find the answer to their question without reading the whole site.

Provenance convention used throughout:

- **Official** = drawn from the FAST-NUCES Academic Rules & Regulations (Prospectus 2025) or the live `nu.edu.pk` pages. Treat as the rule.
- **Reality** = drawn from r/FASTNU lived-experience logs. Treat as how the rule actually plays out.
- Where the two conflict, both are stated and the conflict is named.

> ⚠️ Every fee figure, test date, and aggregate weighting must be re-verified against the live `nu.edu.pk` portal before publishing — these revise annually.

## Sub-Section 1.1 — Admissions Overview & Eligibility

**URL:** `/admissions/overview` · **Title:** FAST Admissions: Eligibility, Streams & How to Apply

### Headings

#### Who Can Apply — Eligibility Criteria

- Eligibility is defined on the central page `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/EligibilityCriteria`.
- A minimum intermediate / FSc / ICS percentage (or O/A-Level equivalence via IBCC) is required. **The exact minimum percentage is set per program and per cycle** — look up your target program on the EligibilityCriteria page above to get the current figure.
- Subject combinations differ per program: CS/AI require the maths-heavy combination (Maths + the relevant science electives); other programs have their own combinations listed on the same page.
- O/A-Level candidates must obtain **IBCC equivalence** before their academic record can be scored.

#### The Three Admission Streams (at a glance)

- **NU Test** — FAST's own entrance exam. Largest seat pool, lowest closing merit. **Recommended default.**
- **SAT (SAT-I)** — separate merit list, smaller self-selecting pool, higher closing percentage.
- **NTS NAT** — smallest reserved seat slice; historically requires a near-perfect score. **Avoid unless your NAT score is near-perfect.**
- Full comparison lives on the *Stream Comparison* page (1.3).

#### Where the Admissions Information Actually Lives

- Six canonical pages to bookmark before trusting anything on social media:
  - Eligibility Criteria · Admission Schedule · How To Apply · Test Pattern · Scholarship · Fee Structure
- **The central `nu.edu.pk` page is the one actually maintained.** Campus sub-sites (Lahore, Islamabad, Chiniot, Peshawar, Karachi, Faisalabad, Multan) are frequently out of date.
- Treat Reddit, WhatsApp, coaching academies, and even this site as commentary that must be checked against the central portal.

#### The Admissions Calendar (shape, not dates)

- Applications open late spring → NU Test sittings across late June–July → results in ~2–3 weeks → first merit chart → fee submission deadline → classes begin.
- Exact dates shift year to year and are published on the **Admission Schedule** page at `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/Schedule`.

## Sub-Section 1.2 — How to Apply: Step by Step

**URL:** `/admissions/how-to-apply` · **Title:** How to Apply to FAST NUCES — Application Walkthrough

### Headings

#### Step 1 — Create Your Application Account

- Register on the central admissions portal at `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/HowToApply`.
- Use a stable email and phone number you check — all correspondence lands here.

#### Step 2 — Choose Programs and Campuses (Preference Ordering)

- You list programs/campuses in order of preference. FAST grants your **first preference if your aggregate clears it**. If your aggregate clears a lower preference but not your first, you are accepted into that lower preference (status: "Eligible"). If it clears none, you are "Denied."
- Order matters: put the most-preferred program first, but only ones you'd genuinely attend — being "Eligible" into a lower preference still binds you to that choice.

#### Step 3 — Upload Documents

- Intermediate/matric result cards (or O/A-Level + IBCC equivalence).
- CNIC / B-Form, photographs, domicile, and any scholarship/FA supporting documents if applying for aid in-flow.
- Re-upload the final result card once issued. FAST admits conditionally pending final results (standard practice) — your seat is confirmed once the final result meets the eligibility criteria.

#### Step 4 — Pay the Application / Processing Fee

- Paid through the portal's designated method; the admission processing fee is **non-refundable** at FAST and every peer institution.
- Keep the payment receipt — you'll need the transaction reference.

#### Step 5 — Select Your NU Test Sitting

- FAST lets you **self-select your test date** within the registration system.
- Later dates = more prep time, so they fill first. Keep checking the portal as dates populate; you don't have to lock a date the instant you pay.
- Once the earliest slots are consumed, only later (or already-crowded) ones remain.

#### Step 6 — Track Results and the Merit Chart

- FAST does **not** publish a ranked merit list. It publishes a **Selected / Eligible / Denied** chart per campus-and-program intersection.
- "Closing merit" numbers floating online are unofficial reconstructions — treat them as estimates, not fact.

## Sub-Section 1.3 — NU Test vs SAT vs NAT: Which Stream to Choose

**URL:** `/admissions/streams` · **Title:** NU Test vs SAT vs NAT for FAST — Which Stream Should You Choose

### Headings

#### The Core Decision: Which Pool Do You Compete In?

- Your single most consequential admissions choice is the stream, because each competes for a different seat pool with a different closing merit.
- **NU Test:** bulk of seats → most forgiving closing merit → safest for the median applicant.
- **SAT:** smaller, self-selecting pool → higher closing percentage → viable only if you already hold a strong SAT (~1250+ is "tough even for A-Level students"; 1300+ opens real possibilities).
- **NAT:** thinnest reserved slice → historically near-perfect required → *"Do not ever go for NTS NAT."*

#### The Aggregate Formulas

- **NU Test / NAT:** approximately 50% entry test + 50% academic record (intermediate + matric, or O/A-Level equivalence).
- **SAT:** approximately 50% SAT-I + 50% (intermediate/A-Level-equivalence + matric); evaluated on a **separate merit list**.
- These splits are the commonly reported formulas. The authoritative weighting for the current cycle is on the **Test Pattern** page at `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/TestPattern`.

#### Closing Merit: What the Numbers Looked Like (recent cycle, unofficial)

- NU Test, Lahore CS: closing aggregate in the low-to-mid 70s (~73% cited one year).
- SAT list: ~85% for CS-adjacent programs, ~86% for AI.
- NAT: near-perfect; swings violently (a ~+10% jump one year, ~−8% the next).

#### The Verdict

- **Default to the NU Test.** Use the SAT only if you already have a strong score and accept the higher-closing separate list. **Avoid NAT for FAST** — easy test, but the seat pool forces a near-perfect aggregate. FAST "prefers its own test" in practice because that is where the seats are.

## Sub-Section 1.4 — The NU Test: Pattern, Sections & Strategy

**URL:** `/admissions/nu-test` · **Title:** The FAST NU Test — Sections, Weighting & How to Prep

### Headings

#### Test Composition

- Four sections: **Advanced Math, Basic Math, Analytical/IQ Reasoning, English.**
- Reported weightings (FAST revises these between cycles; the authoritative current split is on `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/TestPattern`):
  - Advanced Math ~30% · Basic Math ~20% · Analytical Reasoning ~30% · English ~20%.

#### Which Sections Decide Your Aggregate

- For computing applicants, **Advanced Math and Analytical Reasoning are the swing sections.**
- Engineering-background students repeatedly report Basic Math **word problems** as their weakness — the fix is *translation* practice (paragraph of English → equation), not more raw calculation drill.
- Analytical Reasoning is the most efficiently improvable: finite, pattern-recognisable question types; a few weeks of puzzle practice yields disproportionate gains.
- English is steady, not a swing section for most.

#### Sittings and the Self-Selecting Date Mechanic

- Multiple sittings across a date range; you pick your date inside the registration system.
- Later dates are universally preferred (more prep time) → they fill first.
- Keep checking the portal as dates populate; monitor across several days rather than locking in one shot.

#### How to Prepare (the pre-test playbook)

- Start Advanced Math from your intermediate/Calculus textbook; drill word-problem translation specifically.
- Do Analytical Reasoning daily from a dedicated puzzle bank for ~4–6 weeks.
- Sit at least 2–3 full-length mocks under timed conditions; the NU Test is competitive in aggregate, not difficult in content.

## Sub-Section 1.5 — The SAT Stream & the Discontinued Subject Tests

**URL:** `/admissions/sat-stream` · **Title:** FAST SAT Admissions — SAT-II Gone, SAT-I on a Separate List

### Headings

#### SAT-II (Subject Tests) No Longer Exists

- The College Board **discontinued SAT-II in 2021.**
- FAST central policy admits via **SAT-I only**, on a separate merit list.
- Some campus sub-sites have historically still displayed stale SAT-II references and odd thresholds (e.g. a "1500 minimum"). **Assume any odd SAT rule on a campus sub-site is out of date.**

#### How the SAT List Works

- Separate ranking: ~50% SAT-I + ~50% (intermediate/A-Level-equivalence + matric).
- Closes higher in percentage terms than the NU Test list because the pool is smaller and more self-selecting.
- Recent cycle: ~85% CS-adjacent, ~86% AI.

#### When the SAT Stream Makes Sense

- Legitimate parallel play for A-Level students who sat the SAT anyway and scored strong.
- For most domestic applicants without an existing strong SAT, the NU Test remains the rational default — your NU Test score clears the larger pool more easily than your SAT clears the smaller, higher one.

#### Verifying SAT Claims

- Check every SAT-related claim against `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/EligibilityCriteria`.
- When in doubt, **telephone the admissions office** — even university staff are sometimes "not aware of the policies themselves until the last minute," so a direct call is the only way to close the loop.

## Sub-Section 1.6 — Merit, Cutoffs & Why No One Can "Chance" You

**URL:** `/admissions/merit` · **Title:** FAST Merit & Cutoffs — Why No One Can Predict Your Odds

### Headings

#### FAST Does Not Publish a Ranked Merit List

- FAST publishes a **Selected / Eligible / Denied** chart per campus-and-program intersection.
- Every "closing merit" number online is unofficial, reconstructed from self-reported outcomes — treat as estimate, not fact.

#### How to Estimate Your Own Chances

1. Pull 2–3 years of unofficial merit lists (community folders and aggregate calculators circulate widely).
2. Find the closing aggregate for **your specific campus-and-program** combination.
3. Add **+2–3% as a worst-case buffer.**
4. Compare your computed aggregate to that buffered number.
5. Clear it → plausibly safe (never certain). Sit below it → **apply to a backup university immediately.**

#### Why Cutoffs Move Every Year

- **Seat supply changes:** a new campus, a new program (AI arriving at Lahore), or a surge in SAT-scorer admissions all relax NU Test closing merit by enlarging the pool.
- **National passing ratios:** a year with a low intermediate pass rate compresses the academic-record half of everyone's aggregate and can depress closing merits the following cycle.
- For NAT specifically, the swing is violent — a +10% jump one year, −8% the next.

#### The Mental Model

- Your stress will not change the result. The chart is deterministic once the pool is fixed.
- The only productive use of the waiting period is **securing alternatives** — apply to backups, don't post "chance me" threads.

## Sub-Section 1.7 — FAST vs NUST vs GIKI vs LUMS: Comparative Landscape

**URL:** `/admissions/comparison` · **Title:** FAST vs NUST vs GIKI vs LUMS — Fees, Merit & Outcomes

### Headings

#### Where FAST Sits on Price

- FAST is private, markedly more expensive than public-sector (UET, PUCIT, NED) but generally below the elite-private ceiling (LUMS, GIKI) and broadly comparable to NUST's computing tuition.
- All figures revise annually and must be re-verified on each institution's live fee page.

#### Headline Fee Components (recent cycles)

- FAST's own figures live on `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/FeeStructure`; comparator figures are from each institution's published fee page. All revise annually.
- **Admission/processing fee:** FAST (billed at admission, non-refundable) · NUST Rs. 35,000 · GIKI Rs. 75,000 · LUMS Rs. 176,600.
- **Security deposit:** refundable at all four; GIKI transfers unclaimed securities to an endowment after 3 years — claim promptly on exit.
- **Tuition:** FAST ~Rs. 200,000–255,000/semester (program/campus-dependent) · NUST Rs. 197,050/sem · GIKI Rs. 470,000/sem (tuition + accommodation) · LUMS ~Rs. 1.56M Year 1.
- **Per-credit-hour:** matters for repeats/summer — NUST Rs. 8,000 · LUMS Rs. 45,800. FAST's repeat-fee rate is published on `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/FeeStructure`.

#### The Incidental Charges That Bite Mid-Semester

- Late-payment fines, registration suspension, migration fees, transcript charges — these dominate the lived experience of "affordability."
- At NUST: 2% fine on total payable within 15 days; registration suspended on day 16. FAST's analogous incidental charges are listed on `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/FeeStructure`.

#### The Refund Window (uniform across institutions, HEC 2024 framework)

- **100% refund up to day 10 · 80% to day 15 · 60% to day 20 · 50% to day 30 · no refund from day 31.**
- Admission processing fees are non-refundable everywhere.
- Use this calendar as a decision tool: deposit at FAST to secure the seat, recover most tuition if a NUST/LUMS offer lands within the first two weeks.

#### The Strategic Upshot

- FAST = **middle tier on price, top tier on local employer preference.**
- A student who clears FAST merit and absorbs the fee (post-aid) buys the strongest domestic CV filter in Pakistani computing.
- A student whose end goal is a funded master's abroad is usually better served by NUST/LUMS (gentler grading, stronger international pipelines) or a public university (affordable, preserves GPA) — see the *Going Abroad* page (5.2) for the full fork.
- The decision is never "which university is best" — it's "which is best **for my specific end goal at my specific budget.**"

## Sub-Section 2.1 — The Academic Year & Semester Structure

**URL:** `/academics/calendar` · **Title:** FAST Academic Year, Semesters & the Summer Term

### Headings

#### The Academic Year (Official)

- Starts August/September, ends May/June of the subsequent year.
- Two regular semesters: **Fall** (August → December/January) and **Spring** (January → May/June).
- A regular semester = **16 weeks of teaching + 1 makeup week + 2 weeks of final examinations.**

#### Credit Hours and Course Load

- **Theory:** 1 credit hour = 1 hour of lecture per week.
- **Lab:** 1 credit hour = 3 hours of lab work per week.
- Example: a 3+1 course = 3 hrs lecture + 3 hrs lab per week.
- Normal course load is as prescribed in your program's **study plan**, published in the FAST Prospectus and on `nu.edu.pk`.

#### The Summer Semester (optional, special rules)

- Optional, 8–9 weeks, at the Campus Director's discretion.
- Contact hours per week are **doubled** so the full course is taught in half the time.
- **Research/FYP courses are NOT offered in summer.**
- You **cannot register for a brand-new course** in summer. Only two exceptions: (1) an internship required by HEC, and (2) an elective offered by a distinguished scholar/professional only available during summer.
- You **cannot re-register** for a course you already received a letter grade in (including W).
- You can repeat a **maximum of 2 courses** (with labs) in summer.
- Summer courses **cannot be dropped** — fee is neither refunded nor carried forward.
- **No financial assistance / scholarship / fee concession in summer.**
- A summer course runs only if at least **10 students** register.
- Grading is tied to the same course offered in the last regular semester; if your summer average is higher, it's **scaled down** to the regular course's level before grading.

## Sub-Section 2.2 — Grading System: Grades, GPA & CGPA

**URL:** `/academics/grading` · **Title:** FAST Grading — Letter Grades, GPA, CGPA & the Dean's List

### Headings

#### How Courses Are Assessed (Official)

- Performance is assessed during the semester through midterms, assignments, quizzes, case studies, or other methods the instructor chooses.
- A **course outline** with contents and grading criteria is shared at the start of each course.
- **Normally: 50% semester work + 50% final exam** toward the final grade.

#### Letter Grades & Grade Points — BS/BBA

- A+ 4.00 · A 4.00 · A- 3.67 · B+ 3.33 · B 3.00 · B- 2.67 · C+ 2.33 · C 2.00 · C- 1.67 · D+ 1.33 · D 1.00 · F 0.00.

#### Letter Grades & Grade Points — MS & MBA

- A+ 4.00 · A 4.00 · A- 3.67 · B+ 3.33 · B 3.00 · B- 2.67 · C+ 2.33 · C 2.00 · F 1.00.
- Note: MS/MBA has no D-tier; an F still carries 1.00 points (the minimum pass is C, 2.00).

#### Letter Grades & Grade Points — PhD

- A+ 4.00 · A 4.00 · A- 3.67 · B+ 3.33 · B 3.00 · B- 2.67 · F 0.00.
- Note: PhD has no C-tier; the minimum pass is B- (2.67).

#### Special Grades Common to All Programs

- **FA** = Fail due to attendance shortage (0.00).
- **I** = Incomplete (not used in GPA; converts to **F** next semester if requirements aren't completed).
- **W** = Withdrawn (not used in GPA).
- **CN** (continue Research): allowed only once in MS Thesis-II and in each PhD Thesis I/II/III/IV.
- **S / U** (Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory): PhD Thesis work only; not counted in GPA.

#### SGPA vs CGPA

- **SGPA** = weighted average of grade points earned in a semester (weights = course credit hours ÷ total credit hours that semester).
- **CGPA** = weighted average of all courses taken so far.
- **For repeat courses, only the most recent grade points are used** — even if the repeat grade is *lower* than the earlier attempt.

#### Honours Lists

- **Dean's List of Honors:** SGPA 3.50–3.99 in a semester, completed at regular course load. Name displayed on campus and the university website; recognition certificate issued.
- **Rector's List of Honours:** SGPA 4.00 in a semester, regular course load. Certificate + name on website.

## Sub-Section 2.3 — The Ragra: What It Is & How to Survive It

**URL:** `/academics/ragra` · **Title:** The Ragra — What FAST's Academic Pressure Actually Is

### Headings

#### What "Ragra" Means

- Urdu for *grind/press* — the term FAST students use for the sustained, high-frequency academic pressure that defines the weekly rhythm.
- It's not one policy; it's an emergent property of stacked design choices: dense quizzes/assignments starting ~2 weeks in, **two** midterms (Sessional 1 & Sessional 2) plus finals, a grading culture where a non-trivial fraction fails the foundational programming course, and marking that students experience as inconsistent.

#### The Honest Assessment (former faculty)

- The Ragra is real and frequently miserable, but **not** the unmitigated catastrophe that the most despondent posts portray.
- Former faculty (Dr. Nauman Lehcramzai / "recluze"): survivable by doing a few unglamorous things consistently — show up to class, listen, do assignments/labs yourself and on time, show up for exams. "It's not rocket science."
- Reframe it as a **discipline tax**: the system punishes procrastination and rewards consistency. The students who suffer most are not the least talented but the most inconsistent.

#### Where the Critique Becomes Legitimate

- **FAST's grading is materially stricter than peers** — consequences for anyone whose plan needs a high GPA (see the *GPA Economy* page, 2.4).
- **Industry skills are largely self-sourced** — students report the first two semesters teach little directly applicable to industry; the realisation lands around semester 4–5; the internship/job is won through self-study. Not unique to FAST, but FAST combines it with unusually heavy coursework, narrowing the window to bootstrap skills.

#### The Snowball Effect

- A single slipped week snowballs: a missed concept before Sessional 1 doubles the workload before Sessional 2 (relearn old material while new accumulates).
- The pass/fail margin in a hard course can be 2–3 "absolutes" (FAST's internal mark unit).

## Sub-Section 2.4 — The GPA Economy: What FAST Grading Does to Your Record

**URL:** `/academics/gpa-economy` · **Title:** The GPA Economy — Why a 3.0 at FAST Costs More Than Elsewhere

### Headings

#### Layer 1 — The Average

- Student reports (anecdotal; FAST publishes no batch GPA distribution): more than half of a Lahore CS batch gets **below 2.9**; the average sits around 2.5–2.8; a 3.0 is described as "the highest GPA you can score" in the FAST context — not the middling score it is elsewhere.

#### Layer 2 — The Gateway-Course Failure Rate

- Reported: FAST fails ~40% of the batch in **Programming Fundamentals** every year (figure is approximate but explains first-semester trauma).
- **OOP** catches those who barely passed PF without grasping the concepts.
- **Failing a first-semester course is recoverable and sometimes beneficial** if the repeat rebuilds concepts (see the *Failsafes* page, 2.6).

#### Layer 3 — The Institutional Comparison

- The same effort that yields 2.9 at FAST reportedly yields 3.5+ at ITU, LUMS, COMSATS, or UCP.
- Counter-argument: disciplined students clear 3.3+ routinely; former faculty agrees a 3.5 is "not that tough if you follow the discipline."
- **Synthesis:** FAST's GPA is not impossibly low, but it is **reliably lower than peers for a given effort level.** This gap is the single most important input to the abroad-vs-local decision.

#### The Thresholds That Matter

- Most reputable foreign master's programs set a hard **3.0** cut-off.
- **3.3+** with strong core-course grades is widely reported as "good enough" for admission and many scholarships.
- **3.5+** materially improves scholarship odds (was, until the German system changed, the fast-track visa threshold).
- For a local career, GPA matters far less than skills and projects — a 2.9 FAST grad can still land five pre-graduation offers; the tag clears the CV filter regardless.

## Sub-Section 2.5 — Attendance, Missed Exams & Academic Integrity

**URL:** `/academics/rules-attendance` · **Title:** Attendance, Missed Exams & Academic Dishonesty at FAST

### Headings

#### Attendance Requirement (Official)

- Students must maintain **100% attendance**. Absences up to **20% of total lectures** can be condoned for genuine reasons such as illness (this is the official condonation allowance — it is not automatic; you submit the reason and the university approves it).
- **Minimum attendance to sit finals:** 80% for undergraduate, 75% for graduate/postgraduate.
- Falling below the minimum renders you **ineligible to sit the final exam** → you receive an **F/A grade** in that course.

#### Missed Quizzes, Assignments & Exams

- **Missed quiz/assignment** → zero marks. No retake provision for these.
- **Midterm / final exam** → retake is possible **only** for illness, serious accident, or death in the immediate family. A Faculty Committee examines the request.
- If a final-exam retake is allowed, it's held as soon as possible but **not later than one week before the next semester starts.** Grade must be finalised before the next semester. **Retake exam fee is payable** before the exam.

#### Academic Dishonesty (Official)

- Minimum punishment for unfair means in any test/assignment/midterm/final: **zero marks** in that assessment.
- If the instructor refers it to the **Department Disciplinary Committee** → maximum is an **F grade in that course.**
- If the HOD refers it to the **Campus Disciplinary Committee** → F in **all courses that semester**, and possibly **suspension/expulsion.**

#### The Reality: Contesting an Unfair Accusation

- If accused of cheating without proof, escalate graduatedly:
  1. **Calm conversation with the teacher** — confirm severity (full cancellation vs. partial deduction).
  2. **Written application to the HOD** + request **CCTV footage** of the exam hall.
  3. No F can be assigned without proof; the disciplinary committee requires corroborating evidence.
- Pragmatic companion: if the same teacher teaches you again, lay low and score highly so the record speaks for itself.

## Sub-Section 2.6 — Course Registration, Add/Drop, Withdrawal & Repeats

**URL:** `/academics/registration` · **Title:** Course Registration, Add/Drop, Withdrawal & Repeating Courses

### Headings

#### Semester Registration (Official)

- Register in Fall and Spring and pay the current semester fees.
- Register for the **normal course load** (unless on academic warning).
- Procedure: clear all previous dues → first-semester students register through the Academic Office, all others register online → pay current dues in full immediately after registration.

#### Adding or Dropping a Course

- Request within the **first two weeks** of a semester.
- **HOD approval is essential.**
- **You cannot drop any course in your first semester.**

#### Withdrawing from a Course

- **Not allowed in the first semester.**
- Application must be **countersigned by a parent/guardian.**
- Submit to the HOD **before the last day of classes.**
- For courses with an associated lab, withdrawal is **always from both theory and lab.**
- **Fee is not refunded** for a withdrawn course.

#### Repeating a Course (Official + Reality)

- You **must repeat all failed courses.**
- Students under warning must also repeat passed courses where their grade was below the minimum CGPA required.
- You can also repeat a passed course to **improve** your grade.
- **Only the most recent grade counts toward CGPA** — even if it's lower.
- A **repeat count appears on the transcript** for all repeated courses.
- **Reality:** repeating a foundational course (PF is the canonical example) in the very next semester to *rebuild concepts* is one of the clearest success patterns — graduates who did this report higher final CGPAs than classmates who never failed but coasted on the borderline.

#### Final Year Project (FYP) Registration

- FYP is completed in two regular semesters: **FYP-I then FYP-II.**
- To register for FYP-I you must have passed all courses from the first six semesters of the study plan.
- The HOD can allow registration with a deficiency of **one** course; the Director can allow **two**; **more than two → not allowed.**
- Students on academic warning **cannot** register for FYP-I.
- FYP-II is allowed only after successful completion of FYP-I.

## Sub-Section 2.7 — Academic Warning, Probation & Closure of Admission

**URL:** `/academics/warnings` · **Title:** Academic Warnings, Probation & When Admission Closes

### Headings

#### The Warning Threshold (Official)

- A warning is issued at the end of every semester (including summer) if your CGPA is below the minimum required for graduation:
  - **BS/BBA: 2.00 · MS/MBA: 2.50 · PhD: 3.00.**
- A student on warning needs **HOD approval** to register.
- If CGPA is still below minimum after a semester, the **Warning Count increases by one.**
- **Warning Count reaches 3 → admission is closed.**
- If CGPA improves to ≥ the minimum, **Warning Count resets to zero.**

#### Registration Rules While Under Warning

- Must register for all courses graded **F or U.**
- Should register for all courses graded **D, D+, or C-.**
- Should register for **no more than five courses.**
- **Warning Count = 2 → must NOT register for any new course.**
- Parents/guardians are called for a consultative meeting with the HOD.
- Written HOD approval required for **every** add/drop/withdraw.
- Students on scholarships (OSP, HEC, FAST, others) must take the **full load.**

#### The Reality: A Wrecked First Year Is Recoverable

- Later semesters carry enough credit weight to lift the cumulative average.
- Former faculty to a student at 2.53 after a bad first year: *"the CGPA at the end of first year can be improved quite easily in the latter years."*
- Documented trajectory: 1.6 first-semester CGPA → 3.04 by graduation, after the study method was rebuilt (not just more effort within a broken method).

#### When Admission Is Closed

- Failure to restore a suspended registration.
- Failure to re-continue after freezing admission for **two consecutive semesters.**
- Admission obtained on false documents.
- **3rd academic warning.**
- As a result of disciplinary action.
- Student's own request for personal reasons.
- A new admission is closed if an awaited result doesn't meet eligibility criteria.

## Sub-Section 2.8 — Failsafes: Course Repetition, Reviews, Transfers & Semester Freeze

**URL:** `/academics/failsafes` · **Title:** FAST Academic Failsafes — Repeats, Transfers, Freeze & Reviews

### Headings

#### Course Repetition (strategic use)

- Repeat a failed foundational course in the very next semester and use it to **rebuild concepts**, not just clear the grade.
- Only the most recent grade counts; repeat count shows on the transcript.
- Repeat fees apply (billed per credit hour; the current rate is on `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/FeeStructure`).

#### Grade Rechecking / Review

- After a suspect final-exam grade, request to **view the paper.**
- If refused, escalate to the HOD **in writing.**
- FAST marking is sometimes inconsistent — politeness + a paper trail is the correct posture.

#### Semester Freeze (Official)

- Under exceptional circumstances you can take a semester off by requesting the **Campus Director** for a 'Semester Freeze.'
- A **semester freeze fee** must be paid.
- **Freezing the first two semesters (BS) / first semester (MS) is not allowed.**
- Special hardship exceptions for the first semester (with Competent Authority approval): Iddat · Maternity/Delivery · Death in the immediate family · other justified cases.
- **Reality:** freezing costs only about Rs. 2,000 **if done before enrolling in any course.** Freezing **after** enrollment triggers full fee liability — decide cleanly at the start of the term. (The freeze-fee amount and the maximum number of semesters you may freeze are set by the campus administration — confirm the current figures with your campus academic office.)

#### Transfer Between Campuses (Official)

- Considered only on: death of a parent · posting of a parent to that city · marriage of a female student.
- **Not** considered if: under disciplinary punishment / pending disciplinary action / outstanding dues / during the semester.

#### Maximum Time to Complete the Degree (HEC guidelines)

- **BS/BBA: 6 years** (extendable 1 more year with Statutory Bodies' approval).
- **MBA: 3 years · MS: 4 years · PhD: 8 years.**
- Extensions granted only for genuine unavoidable circumstances (Iddat, maternity, death in immediate family, medical condition).
- If readmitted with credit exemptions, the clock starts from the **first** admission date.

#### Readmission (former FAST students)

- Graduate readmission: credits transferable only if earned in the last **5 years** with at least **B-**; CGPA doesn't transfer; MS Thesis credits don't transfer.
- Undergraduate readmission: transferable if earned in last 5 years with at least **C.**
- A former student on disciplinary warning will **not** be readmitted; one closed due to 3rd academic warning is readmitted **only once.**

#### Credit Transfer from Another University

- Must qualify the merit via FAST's admission test for that year/program.
- Relevance/compatibility judged against FAST's own curriculum.
- Exemption cannot exceed **50%** of total degree credit hours.
- No credit for any course graded **below B-** (GPA < 2.67).
- CGPA from the previous institution does **not** transfer.
- MS Thesis credits are **not** transferable.

#### Pre-Medical Transfers

- Pre-medical students from other universities must submit proof of having passed an **additional Mathematics course** plus its detailed syllabus.
- Admission subject to the Admission Committee's assessment.

## Sub-Section 2.9 — Study Strategy: Front-Loading & the Resource Stack

**URL:** `/academics/study-strategy` · **Title:** How to Study at FAST — Pre-Semester, Summer & Resource Stack

### Headings

#### Why Finals Aren't the Real Deal

- Finals can be **less than 50%** of a course's total marks.
- Quizzes + assignments typically carry 20–25 "absolutes" collectively — and the pass/fail margin in a hard course can be 2–3 absolutes, so the continuous-assessment tranche is frequently **decisive.**
- A student who banks steady marks can sometimes pass before the final; a student who ignores the drumbeat and plans to "see in the final" discovers the final alone cannot rescue an empty ledger.

#### The Operational Habits

- **Study for each quiz on its specific topic** — quizzes are diagnostics; skipping one means you discover your gaps only at the sessional, by which point they've compounded.
- **Pay attention in class** — the topics a teacher emphasises live are the topics the quiz tests.
- **Do assignments yourself, at least in semesters 1–2** — the chain is causal: self-done assignments → understood concepts → good quizzes → good sessionals → low-stress finals. Breaking the first link with copy-paste breaks the whole chain.
- **Build a wide social network, not a deep clique** — in university "you want the entire class," because assignments and group projects are collaborative survival.
- **Stay out of controversies and gossip** — social capital is fragile.
- **Forget the paper the moment you hand it in** — ruminating on one exam while the next approaches is a literal opportunity cost.

#### The Pre-Semester Strategy

- Cover ~half of the coming semester's hardest course **before** the semester starts, so when the Ragra descends you're reviewing, not encountering.
- **Before Sem 1 (Programming Fundamentals):** Harvard's **CS50** (free, YouTube/edX) for C/C++ syntax and the compilation mental model. FAST teaches **C++ first** at most campuses (Karachi reportedly starts with C); Python doesn't appear until ~sem 5.
- **Before Sem 2 (OOP, DLD):** the **recluze** OOP playlist (or CodeWithHarry) + a dedicated DLD channel.
- **Before Sem 3 (COAL, DSA):** **Brainmolder** for COAL, **Abdul Bari** for DSA.
- **Before Sem 4 (OS, Databases, AI):** recluze for OS, Brainmolder for Databases, the relevant AI series.

#### The Summer Allocation (by year, not by whim)

- **First summer:** GPA repair and reinforcement — repeat/improve weak courses; do not rest (resting pushes skill-building and internship timelines backward).
- **Second summer:** skill acquisition — pick a domain, learn its stack, begin **Leetcode** (but only after DSA is complete; Leetcode before DSA is frustration without instruction).
- **Third summer:** internships — by now the GitHub holds genuine projects and the CV is shaped.

#### The AI-Tool Rule

- In the first two semesters, use ChatGPT/Claude as **teachers, not authors.** Copy-pasting assignments in the logic-building phase is "skipping two semesters" — it outsources exactly the reasoning the first year exists to develop.
- Later: feed coursework to an AI **before** a course to pre-learn, and **after** to generate practice questions and explanations — reinforce, don't replace.

#### The Canonical Resource Stack

- **CS50** (Sem 1, PF) · **recluze** (OOP, OS, COAL, "How to Approach Learning"/"Boost your BS") · **Brainmolder** (COAL, Databases) · **Abdul Bari** (DSA) · **CodeWithHarry / Apna College** (C++ syntax) · **Organic Chemistry Tutor** (Calculus & Diff-Eq, essential for pre-med converts) · **Simple Snipptes / GATE Smashers** (theory) · a dedicated **DLD** channel.

## Sub-Section 3.2 — Financial Assistance (FA): How It Works

**URL:** `/finances/financial-assistance` · **Title:** FAST Financial Assistance — How FA Works & What It Really Costs

### Headings

#### What FA Is (and Isn't)

- **Need-based, not merit-based** — the percentage scales with documented family income and expenses, not your grades.
- Structured as **Qarz-e-Hasana (an interest-free loan), not a grant.**
- Repayment begins **3 months after graduation or upon securing a job, whichever is earlier**, with the full amount due **within 4 years** of graduation.
- Maintaining FA usually requires not failing courses and holding a CGPA above a floor (commonly around 2.5). The exact maintenance CGPA is stated on the FA form / `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/Scholarship`.
- Apply via the FA form at `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/Scholarship` (and the FA form PDF on the downloads path).

#### The Application Procedure (reconstructed from a 60%-FA recipient)

1. **Apply during admission** — fill the FA form in-flow, or download/submit on campus if you missed it.
2. **Assemble the evidence pack** — FA form + family income payslip + bank statement + latest electricity & gas bills + house info (rented/owned). Supporting docs (disability certificate, report cards) help but aren't required.
3. **Attend the on-campus FA interview** — mandatory, with your father/guardian. The panel asks "each and everything" about how the family manages; honesty and specificity determine the percentage.
4. **Bridge the gap before FA lands** — FA typically awards **mid-semester**, so you must arrange the first installment yourself. An installment plan can reduce the first bill (documented case: Rs. 255,000 → Rs. 150,000 up front, balance later).
5. **Maintain eligibility** — keep CGPA above the floor; avoid fails.

#### The Repayment Arithmetic (model it before accepting)

- ~Rs. 120,000 relief/semester × 8 semesters ≈ Rs. 960,000–1,000,000 total debt → **~Rs. 21,000/month minimum** over the 4-year window after graduation.
- A 25%-FA recipient owing ~Rs. 280,000 reported minimum payments of Rs. 10,000–15,000/month.
- **Takeaway:** FA makes FAST *accessible*, not *cheap* — it converts an upfront impossibility into a post-graduation annuity. If your plan is to leave Pakistan immediately, the repayment schedule can become a leash.

## Sub-Section 3.3 — Scholarships: Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA & Ihsan Trust

**URL:** `/finances/scholarships` · **Title:** FAST Scholarships — Honhaar, PEEF, OSAF, FANA & Ihsan Trust

### Headings

#### Punjab Honhaar Scholarship (government, full-ride by design)

- Flagship government scheme covering the full four-year bachelor's for eligible students; intended 100% tuition (government + institution share).
- **Reality at FAST has been rocky:** the university at one point claimed it covered only 50% and that funds hadn't been received; contradicted earlier statements about which students were on the HEC list; many students were left without award letters while other institutions and campuses (notably Islamabad and Chiniot, where disbursement ceremonies were held) had already distributed cheques.

#### PEEF (Punjab Educational Endowment Fund)

- Separate Punjab-government channel for Punjab-domiciled students with monthly family income below ~Rs. 60,000.
- Apply at the start of the academic year through the university's HR/accounts office.

#### OSAF

- Listed on the FAST website as a further aid instrument; an email circulates after the semester starts inviting applications. See `nu.edu.pk/Admissions/Scholarship`.
- **Caveat:** a student already drawing another scholarship can be ineligible for OSAF — check the exclusivity rule with the campus accounts office before applying.

#### FANA & Ihsan Trust

- Additional need-based channels in the FAST aid ecosystem (Ihsan Trust is an external charitable trust).
- Coverage and eligibility details are available from the campus accounts/FA office.

#### Merit-Based (top-of-test)

- Up to 100% free tuition for top entrants — ace the entry test; top rankers receive full tuition relief. No repayment.

#### The Rule

- **Apply to every channel simultaneously** — FA + Honhaar + PEEF + OSAF + Ihsan Trust. Pools are not always mutually exclusive; the worst case is a delayed partial award, not a rejection. Check which channels can stack with the campus accounts office.

## Sub-Section 3.4 — When You Cannot Afford FAST: FAST vs IST vs Public

**URL:** `/finances/affordability` · **Title:** Can't Afford FAST? — FAST vs IST vs the Public Alternative

### Headings

#### The Representative Dilemma

- A village student, family income ~Rs. 80,000/month, selected for BS Cybersecurity at FAST (Rs. 200,000–250,000/semester + ~Rs. 100,000 hostel), already holding IST CS admission at Rs. 150,000 with no hostel and a possible refund for top-three placement.
- This is the question every financially constrained FAST admit eventually faces.

#### The Consensus (including former faculty)

- Former faculty (recluze), on that case: *"CS in IST would be great for you... People seem to give way too much weightage to the university name. It matters, but not that much. Spend time polishing your skills while you're in your early semesters and you would do great in IST as well."*
- IST is genuinely strong; its load, while heavy, leaves room for a part-time job — FAST's Ragra makes that nearly impossible.
- The financial difference (fee + hostel + incidentals + FA repayment annuity vs. IST/UET/PUCIT/NED outlay), compounded across 8 semesters, can exceed a million rupees — about the size of the FA debt you'd carry into graduation.

#### Program Choice Within Affordability

- **BS Computer Science is a safer, broader choice than BS Cybersecurity** if you're not certain of your path.
- Cybersecurity has thin entry-level job opportunities in Pakistan (largely government/specialised); CS opens the entire software/AI/data landscape and **still permits a later pivot into cybersecurity** — the reverse pivot is far harder.
- Former faculty does "not generally recommend people picking Cybersecurity over CS."

#### The Affordability Decision Tree

1. Can your family absorb FAST's fee *without* aid, or with aid but without borrowing beyond FA itself? → FAST's domestic-career upside is real.
2. If FAST requires external borrowing on top of FA → compare total 4-year outlay (fee + hostel + incidentals + FA annuity) vs. IST/UET/PUCIT/NED/COMSATS.
3. If the gap is large and your goal is a *local* job → the cheaper institution with strong self-built skills frequently wins. The tag clears CV filters, but so does a strong GitHub and a referral; skills dominate the tag after the first interview.
4. If your goal is a *funded master's abroad* → the GPA-preservation argument points you toward the institution with gentler grading (see the *Going Abroad* page, 5.2).
5. **Prefer BS CS over a specialisation when uncertain** — it keeps every door open.

## Sub-Section 5.2 — Going Abroad for a Master's: The Long-Term Fork

**URL:** `/career/abroad` · **Title:** Funded Master's Abroad from FAST — The Honest Fork

### Headings

#### The Case Against FAST (for the abroad path)

- The core claim: FAST's stricter grading leaves you a GPA 0.5–1.0 lower than you'd have earned elsewhere for the same effort — and this gap is *disqualifying* for the abroad path because most reputable foreign master's programs set a hard **3.0** cut-off and competitive scholarships favour **3.5+.**
- The most affecting post: a graduate chose FAST over ITU on crowd advice, graduated 2.9 while his ITU friend graduated 3.6, watched the friend land a fully-funded Finnish master's and settle abroad, and was locked out of the same programs by GPA — *"I applied to over 300 jobs abroad. All rejections. Nobody outside Pakistan seems to recognise or value a FAST degree unlike we praise it here."*

#### The Brand-Recognition Problem

- In foreign master's application dropdowns, NUST and LUMS appear as selectable institutions, IBA semi-regularly, but **FAST is filed under "other"** and is "nowhere to be found on any international rankings."

#### The Balanced Synthesis

- FAST is **not categorically bad** for the abroad path — a disciplined student who clears **3.3+** remains eligible for most good master's programs and many scholarships, and FAST grads *do* reach Michigan, UCL, Belgium, Germany, and occasionally the Ivies (complementing a modest GPA with MOOCs, research, work experience, strong core-course grades).
- The problem is **probabilistic and effort-adjusted:** the same student who would cruise to 3.5+ at NUST/LUMS/GIKI/ITU/a public uni must grind materially harder to reach 3.3 at FAST, and ~90% of FASTians land below 3.5.

#### The Decision Rule

- If the abroad master's is the **primary goal** and you hold a genuine alternative offer (NUST, LUMS, GIKI, ITU, strong public uni), the expected-value calculation frequently favours the alternative — not because FAST can't work, but because it raises the effort cost and lowers the probability.
- If your only alternatives are lower-tier privates (BNU, UMT, UOL), **FAST remains the better choice** — the tag at least dominates the domestic fallback.
- **Never drop a FAST seat for a lower-tier university**; only trade it for a genuinely stronger or more abroad-aligned one.

# MACRO-SECTION 6 — Help & FAQ

## Sub-Section 6.1 — Frequently Asked Questions

**URL:** `/help/faq` · **Title:** FAST Admissions & Academics — Frequently Asked Questions

### Headings (each a self-contained Q&A — perfect for FAQ rich results)

#### Should I take the NU Test or the NTS NAT for FAST?

Take the NU Test. FAST allocates the majority of its seats to NU Test candidates, so the closing merit on that list is the most forgiving. The NAT stream competes for a much smaller reserved pool and has historically required a near-perfect score; its closing merit also swings violently year to year.

#### Does FAST still accept SAT Subject Tests (SAT-II)?

No. The College Board discontinued SAT-II in 2021. FAST admits on the basis of SAT-I on a separate merit list. Some campus sub-sites still display stale references to SAT-II — always verify against the central `nu.edu.pk` admissions pages.

#### What is the minimum attendance required to sit finals at FAST?

80% for undergraduate programs and 75% for graduate/postgraduate. Falling below renders you ineligible to sit the final exam, and you receive an F/A grade in that course. Up to 20% of absences can be condoned for genuine reasons such as illness.

#### What CGPA do I need to avoid an academic warning?

2.00 for BS/BBA, 2.50 for MS/MBA, and 3.00 for PhD. If your CGPA is still below the minimum after a semester, your Warning Count increases by one; when it reaches three, your admission is closed.

#### What GPA do I need to go abroad for a funded master's?

Most reputable foreign master's programs set a hard 3.0 cut-off; a 3.3+ with strong core-course grades is widely reported as "good enough," and 3.5+ materially improves scholarship odds. Hitting those numbers at FAST costs materially more effort than at NUST, LUMS, ITU, or a public university.

#### Is FAST's Financial Assistance a grant or a loan?

It is Qarz-e-Hasana — an interest-free loan, not a grant. Repayment begins 3 months after graduation or when you secure a job (whichever is earlier), with the full amount due within 4 years. At ~60% FA, the total owed is roughly Rs. 1,000,000, implying ~Rs. 21,000/month for four years after you graduate.

#### Can I afford FAST if my family income is around Rs. 80,000/month?

It is difficult and depends on the aid package. Apply to FA + Honhaar + PEEF + OSAF + Ihsan Trust simultaneously, model the FA repayment annuity, and keep a backup university (IST, UET, PUCIT, NED) whose fee you can absorb without aid. If FAST requires external borrowing on top of FA, the cheaper institution with strong self-built skills frequently wins for a local career.

#### Is the "gender rules" viral post about FAST real?

No — it is satire. FAST's actual norms are those of a normal co-educational Pakistani university with customary cultural courtesies. The anxiety it mocks is real, but the rules it describes do not exist.

#### Is BS Computer Science better than BS Cybersecurity if I'm unsure?

Yes, generally. CS opens the entire software/AI/data landscape and still permits a later pivot into cybersecurity; the reverse pivot is far harder. Cybersecurity has a thin entry-level market in Pakistan. Specialise only with a confirmed, researched passion.

#### How long do I have to complete my BS at FAST?

Six years per HEC guidelines (extendable one more year with Statutory Bodies' approval). Extensions are granted only for genuine unavoidable circumstances such as Iddat, maternity, death in the immediate family, or a medical condition.

- **SGPA / CGPA** — Semester / Cumulative Grade Point Average.
- **F/A** — Fail due to attendance shortage.
- **I / W** — Incomplete / Withdrawn (not used in GPA).
- **Dean's List / Rector's List** — honours for SGPA 3.50–3.99 / 4.00 at regular course load.
- **FYP-I / FYP-II** — the two-semester Final Year Project.
- **Warning Count** — increments when CGPA stays below the minimum; 3 → admission closed.

#### Financial Terms

- **FA** — Financial Assistance; need-based, Qarz-e-Hasana (interest-free loan).
- **Honhaar / PEEF / OSAF / FANA** — government and institutional scholarship channels.
- **Qarz-e-Hasana** — interest-free loan structure underpinning FAST's FA.
- **Semester Freeze** — taking a semester off (fee applies; first-two semesters not freezable for BS).
