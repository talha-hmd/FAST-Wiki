How to Apply to FAST NUCES — Application Walkthrough

Applying to FAST is not difficult, but small mistakes in preferences, documents, or test-date selection can create problems later. This page walks you through the application process step by step so you know what to do before submitting your form.

Step 1 — Create Your Application Account

Start by creating your account on the official FAST-NUCES admissions portal. Use an email address and phone number that you actively check, because important updates, test information, and admission-related messages may be sent there.

Do not use a temporary email or someone else's number just to fill the form quickly. Your application account becomes the main place where you track your admission process.

Tip: Keep your login details saved somewhere safe. You may need the same account again for test slips, result tracking, fee challans, or future updates.

Step 2 — Choose Programs and Campuses

FAST asks you to select programs and campuses in order of preference. This order matters because FAST checks your aggregate against your selected preferences. If your aggregate clears your first preference, you may be selected there. If not, you may be considered for a lower preference.

The smart approach is to place your most desired option first, but only include choices you would genuinely accept. Do not add a campus or program casually just because it is available.

Good Preference

You place BS CS Lahore first because that is your real target, then add realistic backup options you would actually join.

Risky Preference

You add random campuses or programs without thinking, then later get eligible for something you never wanted.

Best Practice

Rank your choices based on your real interest, family situation, city preference, budget, and backup plan.

Step 3 — Upload Documents

After selecting your preferences, upload the required documents carefully. These usually include your academic result cards, CNIC or B-Form, photographs, domicile, and any financial assistance or scholarship documents if you are applying for aid.

If you are an O/A-Level student, you will also need your IBCC equivalence because FAST needs your academic record in the local percentage format.

  • Matric result card or equivalent record
  • Intermediate / FSc / ICS result card, or O/A-Level record with IBCC equivalence
  • CNIC or B-Form
  • Recent photograph
  • Domicile, if required
  • Financial assistance or scholarship documents, if applying for aid
Important: Upload clear images or PDFs. Blurry, cropped, or incorrect documents can delay your application or create verification issues.

Step 4 — Pay the Application Fee

Once your application details are complete, the portal will guide you toward the application or processing fee payment. This fee is usually non-refundable, so review your form before paying.

After payment, keep the receipt or transaction reference safe. You may need it later if there is any issue with payment confirmation.

Step 5 — Select Your NU Test Sitting

FAST usually allows applicants to select their NU Test date from available slots. Later dates are often preferred by students because they give more preparation time, so they may fill quickly.

You do not always need to panic-select the first visible date. Keep checking the portal while slots are being updated, but do not delay so much that all suitable options are gone.

1

Complete your application details.

2

Pay the processing fee through the allowed method.

3

Check available NU Test dates on the portal.

4

Select a test date that gives you enough preparation time.

5

Download or save your test slip when it becomes available.

Step 6 — Track Results and Merit Status

After the test phase, FAST announces admission status through its system. FAST does not usually publish a simple ranked merit list like some other universities. Instead, you may see statuses such as Selected, Eligible, or Denied depending on your campus and program preferences.

This is why online “closing merit” numbers should be treated as estimates. They are often reconstructed by students from self-reported results, not officially published ranked lists.

Selected

You have cleared the required merit for that program or campus and can proceed with the admission process.

Eligible

You may be considered for a lower preference or available seat depending on the admission situation.

Denied

Your aggregate did not clear the required level for the selected option in that cycle.

Before You Submit — Final Checklist

Before submitting your FAST application, review everything once carefully. A few extra minutes here can save you from confusion later.

  • Your name and personal details are correct.
  • Your email and phone number are active.
  • Your program and campus preferences are in the correct order.
  • Your documents are clear and uploaded in the right place.
  • Your payment proof or transaction reference is saved.
  • You have selected a suitable test date.

Quick Summary

The FAST application process is simple if you follow it in order: create your account, choose preferences carefully, upload documents, pay the fee, select your NU Test date, and then track your result status.

The most important part is preference ordering. Do not treat it casually, because it can affect where you are selected or marked eligible.
