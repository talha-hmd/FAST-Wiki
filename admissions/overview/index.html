FAST Admissions: Eligibility, Streams & How to Apply

This page gives you a clear starting point for FAST-NUCES admissions. It explains who can apply, which admission streams exist, where the official information lives, and how the admission calendar usually works.

Who Can Apply — Eligibility Criteria

FAST defines eligibility through its central admissions portal. The exact percentage requirement can vary depending on the program and admission cycle, so always verify the latest criteria from the official FAST-NUCES admissions website before making a final decision.

For intermediate students, FAST usually checks your FSc, ICS, or equivalent academic record. For O/A-Level students, IBCC equivalence is required because FAST needs your grades converted into the local percentage system before calculating your academic score.

Subject combinations also matter. Computing programs such as Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, Software Engineering, Cyber Security, and Data Science usually require a maths-heavy background. So before applying, confirm that your subjects match the requirement of your target program.

Important: Do not rely only on WhatsApp groups, Reddit comments, or old screenshots. Eligibility rules can change, so the official admissions portal should be your final source.

The Three Admission Streams

FAST admissions usually happen through three main streams: the NU Test, SAT, and NTS NAT. These streams are not equal because each one competes in a different pool.

NU Test

This is FAST's own entry test. It is usually the safest and most recommended option because most seats are filled through this stream.

SAT

SAT applicants are considered on a separate merit list. This can work well for students who already have a strong SAT score, but the pool is smaller and usually more competitive.

NTS NAT

NAT is generally not the preferred route for FAST because the reserved seat pool is small. Even though the test itself may feel easier, the required score can be very high.

For most applicants, the practical advice is simple: prepare seriously for the NU Test unless you already have a strong SAT score.

Where the Admissions Information Actually Lives

FAST has multiple campus websites, but the central admissions portal is the one you should trust first. Campus pages can sometimes be outdated, especially for admission policies, SAT rules, fee figures, or deadlines.

Before trusting any claim online, check these official areas:

  • Eligibility Criteria
  • Admission Schedule
  • How To Apply
  • Test Pattern
  • Scholarships
  • Fee Structure

Reddit, seniors, coaching academies, and student-made guides can help you understand the reality, but official rules should always be verified from the live FAST-NUCES portal.

The Admissions Calendar

FAST's exact dates change every year, but the overall pattern is usually similar. Applications open around late spring. NU Test sittings usually happen around late June to July. Results and merit status are announced after that, followed by fee submission deadlines and the start of classes.

1

Applications open on the admissions portal.

2

Applicants select their test date and complete the application process.

3

NU Test sittings take place across the announced date range.

4

FAST announces Selected, Eligible, or Denied status.

5

Selected students submit fees before the deadline to secure admission.

Reminder: Do not assume last year's dates will repeat exactly. Always check the current admission schedule.

Quick Summary

If you are applying to FAST, first confirm your eligibility, then choose your admission stream wisely. For most students, the NU Test is the best default option. SAT can be useful if you already have a strong score, while NAT is usually risky because of the smaller seat pool.

The smartest approach is to use student guides for explanation, but use the official FAST portal for final confirmation.
